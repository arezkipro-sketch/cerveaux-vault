## How do you post consistently on social media without burning out?

The easiest way to keep posting on social media without burnout is to lower your standards and build what works for your schedule.

And if you want to show up more often on social media, but you’re wildly inconsistent, please read this.

Because a few weeks ago, something happened that forced me to completely rethink my social media strategy.

**My social media manager quit.**

Now, before you think this blog post is about to get all spicy and dramatic, like a High School burn book… I hate to disappoint you.

But there is absolutely zero tea to spill here and no juicy gossip to share.

She’s an incredible mom who decided she wanted to spend more time with her kids while they are young.

And honestly, I respect that more than anything. I’m so excited for her during this chapter, although I will really, really miss working with her every day.

She was one of my very first hires, worked with me for over six years and helped me build my business into what it is today.

And listen, she didn’t just post content. She helped me brainstorm ideas and hooks, she researched trending topics, she planned my social calendar, and she designed my Reels, graphics and carousels in Canva. And, most importantly, she kept me accountable to showing up consistently, nearly every single day for six years now.

So, needless to say, when she told me she was leaving…

I panicked a little at first, like “omg, what am I going to do?!” but then I made a decision that surprised even myself.

I decided not to hire another social media manager right away.

As the online landscape continues to massively shift and evolve, I decided now was the perfect time to reevaluate my social media strategy from scratch and get my hands dirty again in the place where my business all started.  
  
Right here on social media.

Which means for the first time in six years, I’m running my own social media accounts again. Like I was in the early days, when I was freelancing full-time and starting my own business on the side.

Now, obviously, this time it’s a bit different. I’m not starting from zero followers, and I have years of proven content formulas, templates and processes to refer back to, and my social media manager was an angel for documenting that all for me before she left.

But when it comes to actually showing up, researching topics and trends, creating content and posting consistently, well, it’s all on me again…

Excited to tap back into the creative process, excited to reconnect with my mission, reestablish what I want to say in this new era, and get on the pulse of what’s happening right now, and you better believe it’s going to be scrappy because honestly, scrappy is all I’ve got time for right now.

### Social Media Tip 1: Lower the Bar

The first thing I realized after my social media manager quit was this: My super high standards might be the thing that stops me from posting, and that’s a big mistake.

A lot of people think they need the perfect hooks, perfect lighting, perfect strategy, perfect design… perfect, perfect, perfect.

***But perfection is the enemy of consistency.***

So lately I’ve been asking myself a different question.

Not: “Is this the best piece of content possible?”

But: “Is this good enough for today?”

When you lower the bar, you naturally have a higher posting consistency.

Most content creators will tell you that most of their content gets average views and engagement, and often the stuff they think is going to crush absolutely flops. Whereas the most random post they took two mins to create somehow goes viral.

Stop overthinking it. Get scrappy. Post the ugly, imperfect, awkward thing.

### Social Media Tip 2: Stop Trying to Be Strategic All the Time

I know this might sound ironic coming from someone who teaches marketing.

***But not every post needs to be strategic.***

It’s okay and actually better for some posts to simply be: random thoughts, observations, quick lessons, funny anecdotes or behind-the-scenes snapshots.

When you feel like everything you post has to be high value, you will eventually either run out of ideas or run out of steam. And what happens then?

Yep, you stop posting.

Ironically, a lot of my most viral posts are often the least planned ones.

They were scrappy, unedited, and posted in the moment rather than planned in advance.

### Social Media Tip 3: Talk About What You’re Already Doing

One of the easiest content hacks ever is this: document instead of create.

And no I’m not talking about becoming a Vlogger and constantly recording and sharing your life. We are lowering the bar, not raising it – remember.

So instead of thinking: “What should I post about today?”

***Ask yourself: “What am I already doing or thinking about today?”***

And then share that.

If you’re writing emails: talk about email marketing.

If you’re working on building a new funnel: share a little sneak peak or maybe talk about something you’re learning.

And, if you’re working with clients: share a bit of the process or lessons.

You don’t need to constantly be coming up with new or “viral” ideas.

Social media isn’t about that anymore.

Social media is simply about showing up and letting your people know who you are, what you do, and how you can help them.

And the easiest way to do that is to simply talk about the work you’re already doing.

A one-sentence insight, a quick selfie video in the mirror, a screenshot with commentary, a tweet-style thought.

### Social Media Tip 5: Stop Waiting for Content Batching Days

For years I thought the only way to make posting consistently easy was to do “content batching days”.

Aka – an entire day once every 2-3 months dedicated to just filming video content for social media.

The premise behind it is brilliant… spend one day creating, and get weeks of content.

The problem was… batching was actually a massive pain in the ass.

It involved so many people – my social media manager had to plan out weeks of detailed content ideas. My copywriter had to write the scripts. I had to hire a videographer to record and edit them. Then my team had to review all the videos, ask for edits, do revisions, and approvals. Then of course we can schedule it and post it.  
  
This also often meant content was being posted an entire month after filming it and in Internet time, that feels like a year.

Not to mention, I’d have to spend an entire day in the studio, talking non-stop, countless outfit changes, which meant pure exhaustion, especially if it was a day I wasn’t really “In the mood” to do it.

And, if I’d miss just one batch day, my entire strategy went out the window.

So instead I’ve been doing something simpler.

I just post when I have something to say. Nothing beats “now” energy in marketing. The scrappier, the more relevant, the better. Forget fancy filming, outfit changes, editing – nope. I just post when I have something to say.

Which means I might post three days in a row… and then skip a day.

And for me, that’s okay.  
  
If I don’t feel like being on video, I post a carousel or something else. Cool.

***Consistency doesn’t have to mean that you post every single day.***

Maybe it’s once a week, twice a week, or three times a day. Mix it up. Just don’t post and ghost.

### Social Media Tip 6: Repeat Yourself More Than You Think

One of the biggest myths about content is that you need new ideas all the time.

You don’t.

In fact, your audience probably hasn’t even seen half your content.

Which means you can repeat the same ideas over and over again – in different ways.

The same concept, idea or lesson can be communicated in: a different format, with a different example, or a different story.

I do this all of the time – not just on social media. But in my [emails](https://copyposse.com/#newsletter), [my YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@AlexCattoni) and yes even here on [my blog](http://www.copyposse.com/blog).

You don’t have to recreate the wheel. If a message is important…

If it still rings true and valuable…

***Then it is absolutely okay to say it more than once. In more than one place.***

The other thing that’s important to remember is that people will follow you for more of the same content.

Think of all your fave creators. You likely followed them because you saw one of their signature posts and you want to see more of that.  
  
So one of the best hacks for creating consistently on social media is to create a handful of signature series that you can refer back to all the time, so you’re not reinventing the wheel.

I have a few templated signature series that I post again and again and again – the content is new but the format is rinse and repeat, and they work almost every time.  
  
So test different formats, find the ones that hit, then make that a signature series. Boom easy.

### Social Media Tip 7: Make Posting Easier Than Not Posting

The real key to consistency isn’t motivation, It’s friction.

If posting content means you need at least 30 minutes of time at minimum, perfect lighting, a flawless graphic, and a perfect caption.

You won’t do it. At least, you won’t do it very often.

Start asking yourself “how can I make posting ridiculously easy?”

That might mean you record on your phone and post a raw unedited video. It might mean sharing messy ideas. It might mean you dig up an oldie but goodie and reshare it.

Heck, it might even mean that you repost someone else’s content that hits a valuable point or meaning for you – making sure to share the original poster and give credit, of course.

Because the easier something is, the more likely you’ll actually do it.

And honestly, that is the biggest mindset shift I’m making right now.

For years my content system was built for maximum quality.

But right now my goal is something different.

***Consistency.***

Because the truth is the people who win on social media aren’t the ones with the best strategy.

They’re the ones who show up.
