With this guide I'll give you an overview of the most used copywriting frameworks that definitely will help you to achieve your copywriting goals.

## AIDA

AIDA is one of the most well- known and used frameworks in copywriting.

It’s perfect for:

✅️ Email marketing  
✅️ Landing page  
✅️ Sales pages  
✅️ Ads

The acronym stands for:

- Attention
- Interest
- Desire
- Action

## Attention

The secret to grab readers’ attention is to write catchy and magnetic headlines and great hooks impossible to ignore so they have no other option than to read the first paragraph of your copy.

Here are some examples:

- “How to lose weight without giving up delicious food"
- "The proven strategy to turn your skills into a high-income job"
- “How to turn your blog into a full - time career and become a freelancer in 21 days”

Headlines like these catch your readers’ attention so they feel motivated to know more and keep reading the first paragraph of your copy.

## Interest

Write a strong hook and an engaging first paragraph to capture your readers’ interest and lead them through your copy.

This paragraph is the bridge to the rest of your copy and here you have to engage your readers even further, so they want to read the second paragraph to know more.

## Desire

It's in the second paragraph of your copy that the magic happens. If your readers reach this part of your copy, that's great.

Add bullet points to better highlight the benefits of your product and make it more desirable.

## Action

Action is used in the last part of your copy, where you have to tell your readers the exact action to take.

To persuade people to take action without thinking too much, add urgency in the action step.

For example, a limited time offer or a CTA followed by a countdown.

And to further encourage your readers to buy, add a guarantee so they trust your offer more, feel safe and click the buy button.

The most used guarantee is the “money back guarantee”.

In the “action” step, CTA buttons come into play to guide readers to complete the purchase.

That’s the AIDA framework.

## A Quick Note About CTA Buttons

Start your call to action with a strong verb ( Buy now, Subscribe, Sign up, etc. ).

Be direct and if you can, address your reader in your CTA by using the second person. For example, “Download Your Free Ebook”, “Save Your Seat”, “Secure Your Spot”, etc.

Also the color of your CTA button matters and it must be in contrast to the rest of your content to be visible and clear for your readers.

You can choose any colors you want, such as blue, green, or orange. But be sure to use colors that match your brand color palette for brand consistency.

An essential thing to keep in mind is that every color has its own meaning and influences people's behaviors.

## AIDCA

In this guide it can't miss the AIDCA framework. It's a modern and expanded version of the AIDA framework with an additional step, Conviction.

It’s useful when you are dealing with skeptical readers and need to build trust to persuade them to buy.

AIDCA stands for:

- ATTENTION
- INTEREST
- DESIRE
- CONVICTION
- ACTION

Attention, Interest, Desire, and Action have the same use of the AIDA framework.

The new entry is Conviction, the step where you have to convince doubtful readers that your product is what they need.

Use social proofs, data, infographics, etc., to share proof of satisfied people who have already used your product or service.

That’s a powerful way to persuade your reader honestly.

## The 6+1 Framework

You can use this framework as a substitute for the AIDA model.

It was used for the first time by Danny Iny to highlight how important the context is to write a powerful copy.

You can use it to write effective emails and sales copy.

Here is the formula in detail:  
CONTEXT ➡ It’s an important step to engage your readers and be sure that your email will be opened and your sales message will be read.

If your readers know who you are and why your message will be important to them, you have more possibilities that your emails won't end in the trash folder.

**ATTENTION** ➡ Writing an attention-grabbing headline is key to engaging your readers and drawing their attention.

**DESIRE** ➡ In this step, writing a compelling hook is key to lead your readers from thinking about your product or service to a strong desire to have it.

**THE GAP** ➡ Present your product and show your readers what can happen if they don’t take action and what can happen if they do.

Stress the effect of not taking action, so they start reflecting on the two scenarios.

**SOLUTION** ➡ Now position your product or service as the perfect solution to your readers’ problem and lead them toward the call to action step.

**CALL TO ACTION**: It's an important stage of your copy because you have to lead your reader to a decision.

So your message has to be direct and clear. For example:

- “Join our community”
- “Enroll today and save 50%”
- “Download your ebook”
- "Buy now and save big”

The additional step, the +1 is crucial to establish “ **credibility** ”. What does it mean? It means that people need to trust you, they need to be sure you really understand them and that you have a real interest in solving their problem.

And above all, they must be sure that your product is the right fit to change their situation.

So, provide social proof to establish credibility and earn your readers’ trust.

End your copy with a guarantee so they can feel safe and more willing to buy.

## PAS

It's a simple but powerful framework for every kind of copy.

The acronym means:

- Problem
- Agitation
- Solution

Problem 👉 show your readers the problem they have.

Agitation 👉 stress the problem strongly enough to have a deep emotional impact on your readers.

**Solution** 👉 provide your readers with the perfect solution to the problem highlighted in the previous stages. This solution is your product or service.

As a rule of thumb, to write an effective copy you have to study your target audience and take into consideration which [stage of awareness](https://bit.ly/4qw02z4) they are in, so you can write the right message to the right people.

This is super important especially in the agitation step.

## The 4 C’s Framework

It’s a powerful framework to write compelling copy.

It stands for:

- Clear
- Concise
- Compelling
- Credible

**Clear** ➡ ️ To draw people’s attention be sure you’re writing in a way they easily understand what you are saying.

Avoid jargon or complex sentences. There is a reason behind the quote “Write like people talk”. People need simple words.

So if you want people to pay attention to you, follow this quote and keep your copy simple.

**Concise** ➡ Don’t beat around the bush, go straight to the point, you don’t need unnecessary words or other complicated structures to explain something.

People’s attention span is short, especially today, with our fast-paced life and a world full of distractions.

**Compelling** ➡ People are emotionally driven. So use storytelling, valuable and engaging content to persuade people to click the CTA button. But be honest about your offer if you want people to come back to you.

**Credible** ➡ People buy when they trust you or your product. The best way to establish trust is by adding testimonials and reviews from happy customers who have already used your product or service.

It’s ideal to write B2B copy.

## P. A. S. T. O. R

This is a Ray Edwards formula, a master copywriter, who explained it in detail in his book "How to Write Copy That Sells", a powerful and super useful educational book.

It stands for:

- Person, Problem, Pain
- Amplify and Aspirations
- Story and Solution
- Transformation and
- Testimony
- Offer
- Response

## Person, Problem, Pain

This is one of the most relevant parts of your copy.

Knowing your target audience (**person**) is key to writing a focused message.

Then you have to figure out the **problem** they have and that causes the **pain**.

## Amplify and Aspirations

In this stage, you have to show your readers what happens if they don't take action “right now” and miss the opportunity. (**amplify**)

Then highlight what is going to happen if they take action and buy your product.

So they start thinking about your product as the solution to their problem and want to have it.

Now they feel motivated because they have a solution and can aspire to have a brighter future ahead. (**aspiration**)

## Story, Solution and System

The next step is storytelling.  
So tell a story. But it must be a real and inspirational story on how someone else overcame the same problem with your product as the **solution**.

To convert readers into returning customers you need to earn their trust, both in you as a brand and in your product.

No fake stories, because if you cheat your readers once you will lose them in the future. Be honest.

Then, describe the **system** that helped other people solve the problem.

## Transformation and Testimony

**Transformation** is what your readers are looking for from your product or service.

Prove the effectiveness and reliability of your product in the **testimony** step by providing testimonials from satisfied customers to show how it transformed their lives.

## Offer

In this part of your copy you have to talk about your product or service.

Follow the 80/20 rule.

Talk about your readers and their problem, the results and the transformation of your existing customers for the 80% of your copy, and only in the 20% you can talk about your offer.

## Response

This step is where you tell your readers the action to take to get your product or service.

Be direct, concise and compelling.

## AIDPPC

It's a new version of the classic Aida framework. You can use it to sell high - ticket offers.

It stands for:

**ATTENTION**: Grab the readers’ attention with a catching headline.

**INTEREST**: Make your offer interesting and engaging.

**DESIRE**: Talk about your product in a way your readers want to have it.

**PERSUASION**: Persuade your readers with an irresistible offer. Leverage the scarcity and urgency principles to increase the power of persuasion. For example, by using limited time offers.

**PROOF**: Share social proof to show how your product helped other people so your readers will trust you.

**CLOSE**: be clear and direct and tell your readers what to do next so they know what action they have to take and click the buy button.

## ACCA

If you have to sell high - ticket products or services, like SaaS or financial services or if you are a non- profit or charity organization and you have to ask for donations, this framework is for you.

You can use it for landing pages or emails to write content that educates your readers about your offer and leads them to problem awareness to taking action.

It stands for:

- AWARENESS
- COMPREHENSION
- CONVICTION
- ACTION

**Awareness** 👉 write headlines that grab readers’ attention, so they become aware of the problem.

**Comprehension** 👉 delve deeper into the problem and how it is influencing their life.

Then present your offer as the right solution for that problem.

**Conviction** 👉 provide detailed information about your offer. Share proof and data to demonstrate the product’s effectiveness, so they know exactly what they get if they buy your product.

That helps you to build trust and make your product more desirable.

**Action** 👉 write compelling and direct CTA to tell your readers what action they have to take next.

## The 4 P’s:

Let’s break it down:

**PICTURE**: Use attention-grabbing images.

**PROMISE**: What are the benefits of your product and how it can solve the readers’ problem.

**PROVE**: Show social proof.

**PUSH**: You have to push your readers to hit the CTA button and buy your product without being salesy.

## The Slap Framework

This framework is perfect for social media copy or for selling low-ticket offers, especially online.

It means:

- Stop
- Look
- Act
- Purchase

Write attention - grabbing headlines that make people *stop* at what they are reading.

Then present your offer highlighting the benefits with engaging content, video, testimonials adding urgency, so they can *look* and decide to buy.

End by adding a simple and direct CTA to avoid people overthinking so they can *act* fast and click the buy button to complete the *purchase*.

## UPWORDS

You can use it for emails, landing pages, and every kind of persuasive copy.

It means:

**Universal Picture Words Or Relatable Descriptive Sentences**

It's a formula that uses pictures and words together, like metaphors, stories, analogies, etc., to convey a stronger message helping readers identify the problem and visualize it through related images in a persuasive and engaging way.

In other words, images help readers relate the problem to their current situation.

Then, in their mind your offer becomes their solution to change it and they are more willing to hit the CTA button.

## The QUEST Framework

It stands for:

- Qualify
- Understand
- Educate
- Stimulate
- Transition

**Qualify**: a rule of thumb to write effective copy is that you have to research and know your audience before writing your message.

In the “qualify” stage it’s essential to understand [the customers’ stages of awareness](https://bit.ly/3ZuBuer), so you can be sure you’re talking directly to the right audience.

**Understand**: to write copy they want to read and trust, you have to “wear your readers’ shoes” and show them a real interest in understanding their problems and needs so they can relate to your words.

Empathize with your readers because when they feel seen and understood they keep reading.

Then “ **Educate** ” them with valuable and detailed info about the solution and the features of the product to help them see you and your product or service as the best solution on the market.

**Stimulate**: it means you have to use your words or even visual content to arouse curiosity and spark your readers’ interest in your offer.

You can write about the benefits, compare your product with your competitors, maybe highlighting what they might lose if they don't buy the product.

Then in the **Transition** step, you have to guide your customer through the last and most important part of the buying process: deciding if your offer is worth the money and give it a try by hitting the CTA button.

## BAB

It means:

- Before
- After
- Bridge

This framework is used when you have to talk about a product or service and you have to show the transformation comparing the before and the after.

Here is an example…

You want to promote a new product, “Body Slim”, a slimming body lotion. (It’s just an example for this article).

You can show to the readers an image or video with a body before using "Bodyslim lotion" and the same body after its usage.

In the bridge step, you have to show the process that led the person who used the product to that result. Then, persuade the readers with the promise that they also can have the same result.

Claim your promise with proof, testimonials and data to build trust.

For the example mentioned above, you could say:

“ *Tested on 57 women for 30 days with an 89% reduction in abdominal fat.*

*If you want professional results at home for half the price Bodyslim lotion is what you need.*

*Here is the secret to make your wish come true: a long massage every day for 30 days and you'll be amazed by the extraordinary results!*

*And if you’re not satisfied with the results, you can return the product and have your money back even if you have used it.”*

## ACC

You can use it for brand storytelling. It’ s direct and short but if you use it the right way it’s effective.  
The acronym stands for:

- Awareness
- Comprehension
- Conversion

**Awareness** ➡ highlight a problem your audience has.

**Comprehension** ➡ present your product or service as the perfect solution highlighting features and benefits.

**Conversion** ➡ encourage and persuade your audience to click the CTA button.

## 1-2-3-4 Framework

It's a persuasive copywriting formula and you can use it for a lead generation strategy.

This framework answers four simple questions to be sure your copy has covered all the essential elements you need to write copy that converts.

In detail:

1 》What is the offer for your readers?  
Present your readers your product or service.

**2 》What’s in it for them?**  
Show the benefits of your product or service and how it can solve their problem.

**3 》Who are you?**  
Talk about you to create trust and generate leads, but from the readers’ perspective.

Explain why your service or product is the perfect match to solve their problem.

**4 》What’s their next move?**  
This is the CTA section.

Directly tell your readers the action you want them to take.

Add [persuasion's principles](https://bit.ly/3Od04yi) to write converting CTA’s.

## FAB

It’s perfect for email marketing, e-commerce and B2B copy.  
FAB stands for:

- Features
- Advantages
- Benefits

**Features** ➡ What your product or service has: all the technical characteristics.

**Advantages** ➡ What your product or service does and how it works.

You can compare your product with competitors’ products, highlighting the reasons why your product is better.

**Benefits** ➡ What people get with your product or service and how it can impact their life.

Remember, people don’t buy a product, they buy solutions, a transformation, a pain relief. So show them that with your product they get what they need.

## The 3’ S Framework

You can use it to draw people’s attention, influence them and build trust by showing them that your product is the best solution to their problem.

The 3’S stand for:

- Star
- Story
- Solution

Star ➡ Start talking about someone else who overcame a problem with your product or achieved the same transformation your readers want to achieve to catch their attention and spark their interest, so they are motivated to keep reading your copy.

Story ➡ Storytelling is key here. Tell success stories on how other people went through the same problem they have and how they overcame it.

It’s a way to connect with them on a deeper level and make them feel understood.

**Solution** ➡ In this step you have to present your product or service as the one solution they need to reach the same result and persuade them to buy.
