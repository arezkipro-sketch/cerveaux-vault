**Post updated**: 05.01.2026.

As the internet becomes flooded with more and more AI-generated content, it’s getting harder to find copywriting advice that’s genuinely worth your time. Search results are cluttered with generic posts, often written by people who’ve never worked with clients or written a line of copy outside of their own blog.

So, I put this guide together to shine a light on the copywriters who are worth listening to. Every copywriting blog featured here is written by a working copywriter with real-world experience. These writers share lessons from client projects, reflect on the challenges of the job, and offer ideas that come from doing the work.

Some of the blogs focus on the business of freelancing. Others explore brand messaging, UX writing, or conversion copy. What they all have in common is that they offer practical, thoughtful guidance.

If you’re [learning copywriting](https://brandnewcopy.com/how-to-learn-copywriting/), running your own business, or simply looking to improve your skills, these are the blogs worth subscribing to in 2025.

## What Makes a Great Copywriting Blog in 2026?

There’s no shortage of writing advice online, but not all of it comes from a place of real experience. For this list, I looked for blogs that go beyond surface-level tips and actually help you [think like a copywriter](https://brandnewcopy.com/free-copywriting-course/). I’ve also included copywriting blogs that I personally read.

Here’s what I was looking for:

- **Blogs written by working copywriters themselves, not big companies**

Every blog listed here is run by someone who earns a living writing copy.

- **Articles focused on strategy as much as writing technique**

In think the best blogs connect writing with positioning, audience insight, and business goals.

- **Original, practical, and refreshingly human**

In these blogs, you’ll find ideas that feel grounded and examples that show how real writers solve real problems.

## How to Get the Most from Copywriting Blogs

Reading blogs like these is a great way to keep your thinking sharp. They offer different perspectives, practical advice, and reminders that you’re not the only one figuring things out as you go.

But they’re not a shortcut to becoming a better copywriter.

Skills come from doing the work – writing, editing, taking feedback, and learning from your own projects. Blogs can help you along the way, but they’re most useful when paired with practice and a clear learning path.

So, pick a few that align with where you are right now and subscribe to them. Let them supplement your learning, not replace it.

## FAQs About Copywriting Blogs

### What makes a good copywriting blog?

A good copywriting blog shares practical advice grounded in real client experience. It’s clear, readable, and written by someone who actually does the work.

### Are copywriting blogs still relevant in the age of AI?

Yes, if you read the right ones. While AI-generated content clutters the internet, blogs written by working copywriters offer insights you won’t get from generic advice or SEO-driven posts.

### Can reading blogs help me become a better copywriter?

Absolutely, but only if you apply what you learn. Blogs are useful for picking up tips, refining your thinking, and learning from other writers’ experiences. They’re a supplement, not a substitute for doing the work.

### Should I start my own copywriting blog?

If you have something to say and want to build your credibility, yes. A well-written blog can show how you think, attract clients, and help you clarify your own process.

### What’s the difference between a copywriting blog and a content marketing blog?

Copywriting blogs tend to focus on persuasive writing, strategy, freelancing, and client work. Content marketing blogs are usually geared toward SEO, traffic generation, and long-term brand building. There’s overlap, but the mindset and goals are often different.

### What are the best copywriting blogs for beginners?

If you’re just getting started, look for blogs with an approachable tone and clear, actionable advice. Nicole Bianchi’s blog is great for building foundational skills and understanding writing mechanics. Copy School’s blog also covers key principles in a way that’s accessible without being oversimplified.

### Which copywriting blogs cover conversion and UX writing?

Copyhackers is a go-to source for conversion-focused advice, with in-depth articles on testing, landing pages, and user journeys. The Clever Copywriting School blog also touches on UX writing, tone of voice, and content structure, all with freelancers in mind.

### Can I learn copywriting from blogs alone?

Blogs are a great place to start. They can help you build your understanding, get inspired, and stay up to date. But real progress comes from writing, editing, and working on projects. If you’re [serious about learning copywriting](https://brandnewcopy.com/copywriting-course/), you’ll need to combine reading with structured learning and hands-on practice.
