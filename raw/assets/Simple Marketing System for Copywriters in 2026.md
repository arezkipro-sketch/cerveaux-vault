## How do I get clients as a copywriter without posting more and doing more outreach?

Focus your posts on evidence not effort because clients want to see proof that you can do what you say you can.

Now, let me tell you the biggest mistake new copywriters and marketers make that stops them from building successful freelance businesses. And no, t’s not bad writing. It’s not a lack of skills or experience. It’s not even over-reliance on AI.

The reason most copywriters and marketers stay stuck and struggle to land clients is that they completely misunderstand… Marketing. I know, ironic right?!

But it’s not what you think.  
  
I’m not talking about learning marketing so you can better serve your clients. Of course, that is important.

But what I’m talking about is marketing yourself.

**Yup, the biggest mistake you can make as a new copywriter is not treating your business like a business.**

Learning the actual skills is only the beginning. Once you learn the skills, you gotta learn the “business” of writing or any other service you provide.  
  
It’s like learning how to swim.

Sure, you start by learning the breast stroke, and then back stroke and the butterfly stroke.  
  
But once you learn the mechanics of how to swim, the only way to become a swimmer is to get in the freaking pool.  
  
This is about experience, repetition and following proven systems.  
  
The problem is most new copywriters jump in the pool, doggie-paddle for a few seconds, then jump out and say, “yeah, this whole swimming thing just isn’t for me,” or “it’s too hard,” or “the pool is too crowded” before ever really giving it a shot.

Now I’m not saying it’s easy, I’m not saying you won’t struggle from time to time, but just like learning how to swim, building a successful freelance copywriting and marketing business is a fairly simple and predictable process, if you follow the steps and don’t give up.

But first, I need to talk about a giant myth I often hear from people teaching others how to find clients.

### The Biggest Myth About Getting Clients

A lot of new copywriters think landing clients is all about visibility.

“If I just post more on social media…”

“If I just send more cold outreach emails…”

“Or if I just slide into more client DMs…”

And don’t get me wrong, visibility is great. Important even. And I don’t disagree that the more you do this, the more likely you are to eventually get a bite.

But today, in this age of AI, where everyone distrusts everything they see online, visibility without credibility is becoming less and less effective.  
  
And anyone teaching client-getting strategies without addressing this law of diminishing returns is wasting your time.

Listen, cold email outreach can work, social media strategies can work, sliding into a stranger’s DMs can work.  
  
But they work a hell of a lot better if those potential clients trust you and actually take you seriously.

And following up with them 100 times isn’t going to change that.

Because today, clients don’t hire based on **effort**. They hire based on **evidence.**

And there are 3 types of “proof” that your clients look for before they’ll even consider hiring you.

### Marketing Proof #1: Proof of Skills

In the age of AI when anyone with a ChatGPT account can call themselves a “copywriter” (hard eye roll), it has never been more important to showcase your skills in a portfolio.

I’m talking about things like… writing samples, mock landing pages, email sequences, case studies, funnel breakdowns, or anything else that showcases your work to potential clients.

This is what answers the client’s first question: “Can this person actually do the work I need from them?”

But here’s the mistake beginners make. They think a portfolio has to come from paid clients.

It doesn’t. **Some of my most successful students have landed absolute dream clients by simply sending them a rewrite of their existing ads or their sales page.**

This is the fastest way to grab a potential client’s attention, demonstrate expertise and look professional.

![copywriting marketing proof 1 proof of skills](https://i0.wp.com/copyposse.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/copywriting-marketing.png?w=1200&ssl=1)

copywriting marketing proof 1 proof of skills

But skill alone isn’t enough. And that brings us to the next type of proof you need.

### Marketing Proof #2: Proof of Humanity

Again, thanks to AI, this one is absolutely critical, yet THIS is the type of proof that most freelancers completely overlook.

Remember, clients don’t just hire skills.

They’re hiring you, a person.

And this person is someone they’re going to have to message with back and forth, collaborate with, and trust with their business.

So if they can’t get a sense of who you are, they will always default to someone who feels more trustworthy. Even if that other person doesn’t have a portfolio that’s as impressive as yours.

This is why personal branding matters so much.

And it’s not about forcing yourself to post “valuable content” or turn into some sort of fake influencer.

This is about being present, approachable and available when your dream clients come lurking.  
  
**And then, this is a big one: showcase your personality in your communications with future clients.**  
  
AI is making all messaging so watered down, generic, boring and beige. If you want to stand out to potential clients, be you.

This is what makes someone feel like they already know you. I once hired a writer on my team because of the amount of GIFs she used in her email to me. It made me laugh out loud and I appreciated her humor so much, because she was talking to me like a person, and I honestly felt like I knew her already.

When people feel like they know you, they trust you faster.

Now, once you have proof of skills and proof of humanity, you unlock the final piece.

### Marketing Proof #3: Proof of Process

**Once you connect with future clients, it’s really important to guide them through a process.**  
  
Even if you’re brand new and have never actually worked with a client before, showing them that you know what you’re doing and leading them step-by-step through the entire process, will make them feel safe, heard and understood.

This is the difference between being “just” a copywriter who waits for opportunities. And being a real “business owner” who goes out there and does the damn thing like the professional you are.

From scheduling the first call to receiving your first payment, the more prepared you are, the more confident you appear and the more you give off the “this isn’t my first rodeo” vibe (even if it is), the more likely your clients are to hire you, and then hire you again and again and again.

And it starts from the very first bite or interaction you have with your future clients.

![copywriting marketing system 3 proof of process](https://i0.wp.com/copyposse.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/copywriting-marketing-system.png?w=1200&ssl=1)

copywriting marketing system 3 proof of process

There are three systems you need to master as a copywriter and freelance business owner.

#### Marketing System #1 — Attraction

This is all about planting seeds and driving strategic conversions with people already within your network.

I’ve said it before, and I’ll say it again – not everyone in your life is a potential client. Obviously. But everyone, everyone is a potential referrer.  
  
Understanding how to connect with potential advocates, show up on social media and attraction client opportunities is one of the most potent systems to learn as a new business owner.

#### Marketing System #2 — Outreach

This is about how to build your network with people you don’t already have in your circle.

Outreach gets a bad reputation.

But there is a difference between good outreach and spamming people on the internet.

Think of it this way…

Good outreach should always be helpful.

Things like personalized audits, quick marketing suggestions, mock-ups of improvements.

When done well, it positions you as someone who already understands their business and is prepared to help them meet their goals.

And when you go in with proof of skills, proof of humanity and proof of process, you’re miles ahead of everyone else.

#### Marketing System #3 — Onboarding

This is the system many freelancers forget about.

Once a client shows interest, you want a quick and professional way to move them forward from “bite” to “onboarding”.

And if you aren’t already prepared with a process, then you’ll be fumbling back and forth trying to figure out the next step or, worse, appear a little sloppy to your future client, which can break trust.

You need to be ready to immediately offer them the next step in working with you or you risk losing them before you really land them.

Remember, marketing yourself doesn’t end when someone responds.Iin fact, it never ends. How you do anything is how you do everything.  
  
Btw, I recently recorded an entire [video on 5 one-liners](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=laWpFj3LQPY) to make you instantly more trustworthy on client calls.

The best news is none of this is actually complicated.

You don’t need thousands of followers, fancy funnels, viral content, or even a website, all you need are these 3 systems.

And once they start working together, your efforts start compounding.

You build credibility. You create authority. And your outreach starts converting faster.

And suddenly, landing clients stops feeling like a mystery. In fact, it becomes something you never have to worry about or even think about again.

Because clients start coming to you.
