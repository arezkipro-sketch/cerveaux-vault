# Frustration et désir — Summary

**One-liner:** Buying happens when the prospect feels the gap between now (frustration) and the wanted future (desire); the bigger the felt gap, the more likely the action.

## Key takeaways
- **Frustration = exit energy** ("je n'en peux plus"); **Desire = pull energy** ("je veux ça à la place"). The copywriter puts words on both.
- **The trap:** pressing only on pain / dramatising / guilt-tripping → prospect closes. Good copy *clarifies* what they already feel, doesn't manipulate.
- **Use frustration:** identify the exact, specific, lived frustration in the prospect's words; prove understanding ("c'est moi"); show real impact (no exaggeration); expose the true cause (why past attempts failed) → install your angle.
- **Activate desire:** make an improved situation visualisable, concrete/measurable, backed by proof.
- **6-step arc:** Frustration → Impact → Cause → Desire → Bridge (method) → Action.
- **3 mistakes:** too much frustration / too little desire; desire too big & not credible; no concrete bridge.
