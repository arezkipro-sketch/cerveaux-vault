# Trouver son USP — Summary

**One-liner:** "Le marché ne vous rejette pas, il vous confond." A USP isn't a clever tagline — it's a fixed decision that makes you *legible*, not unique; an 8-step method to formulate one sentence that makes you chosen.

## Key takeaways
- **Core reframe:** being "good" no longer suffices in a saturated market of interchangeable profiles; the problem is *indecision*, not confidence. USP = a point fixe that cuts the noise.
- **8-step method:** (1) pen + paper, 3-min freewrite on "why would someone choose ME today" (2) list everything you do, cross out 80% — strike anything a competitor could say verbatim (3) name the *real* painful problem (the unspoken one, not the presentable one) (4) link your difference to that problem ("Je suis le seul à faire X parce que mes clients ont surtout peur de Y") (5) compress to one cutting sentence: **"J'aide [profil précis] à [résultat clair] sans [frustration principale]"** — no "et", no soft adjectives (6) test on ONE real target person ("tu comprends ce que je fais et pour qui?") then shut up (7) place it where it sells (one spot) (8) visualise a first client saying "c'est exactement ça".
- **Mantra:** a USP plants a stake in the ground — exclude part of the market to be chosen by the right people.
