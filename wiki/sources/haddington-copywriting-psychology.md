# The Power and Psychology of Copywriting — Summary

**One-liner:** Persuasive copy works by tapping emotions, cognitive biases and social influence — buyers decide on how a product makes them feel, then justify with logic.

## Key takeaways
- Core buying motivations: prevent/ease pain, give pleasure, gain social status or acceptance. Write copy that names one of these.
- **Emotional triggers > features.** "It's often less about the product itself, and more about how it makes them feel."
- Proven persuasion levers: **social proof** (testimonials, reviews, "Join 60,000+ customers"), **scarcity/FOMO** (limited stock, countdown, "ends at midnight"), **reciprocity** (free guide/voucher upfront), **power words** (discover, unleash, proven, limited).
- **Ethics layer (hard rule):** never exaggerate, acknowledge limitations, focus on genuine achievable benefits. Buyers detect manipulation fast; deceptive claims damage the brand.

## Notable claims (with locations)
- "Humans are emotional creatures. Even the most logical decision is often guided by underlying emotions." — *(Emotional triggers)*
- "Scarcity heightens perceived value." — *(Crafting irresistible copy)*
- Example proof-led copy: "97% of users reported healthier skin in 30 days." — *(examples)*
