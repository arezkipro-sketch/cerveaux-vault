# llms.txt : à quoi sert ce fichier — Summary

**One-liner:** llms.txt is a root file that tells generative AI how to read/prioritize/cite your content — a "piloting tool, not mass blocking" that bridges traditional SEO and GEO; NP Digital frames it as a strategic lever (more bullish than the "no AI reads it yet" camp).

## Key takeaways
- **llms.txt = directives for generative AI** (vs robots.txt for search crawlers): which pages may be analyzed, which to prioritize, which uses are allowed (summary/citation/training). Placed at site root.
- **Purpose = reduce ambiguity.** LLMs read blocks + relevance signals; without directives they ignore key pages, misread hierarchy, lean on secondary content. llms.txt steers AI to strategic content. "Piloting tool, not mass blocking."
- **Blocking ≠ disappearing** — it means leaving the LLM's field of view: blocked pages aren't analyzed, aren't cited, stop feeding your expertise signal. You can rank on Google yet be invisible in AI answers. **Risk = oblivion, not penalty.** Block legal/obsolete/low-value pages, never pillar content.
- **SEO role:** a bridge between SEO and [[generative-engine-optimization]]; amplifies (doesn't replace) good SEO.

## When to use (with locations)
- Expert/educational content; brand wants to control how it's cited; competitive environment; future-proofing for answer-engines *(§Quand faut-il)*.

## Implementation principles (with locations)
1. Place at root → 2. **Precise, not aggressive** (prioritize > exclude) → 3. Highlight strategic content → 4. Align with editorial structure (it amplifies existing hierarchy) → 5. Test + adjust *(§implémenter)*.

## Content-structuring tips (with locations)
- **Dense context** (one main idea/section, clear definitions, logical links), **answer-importance hierarchy** (key answers first → [[aeo]]), explicit **tone of voice**, **entity mapping** (LLMs reason in entities not keywords → reinforces [[e-e-a-t]]) *(§Conseils pratiques)*.
