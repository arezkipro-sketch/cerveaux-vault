# First Copywriting Client Panic — Summary

**One-liner:** Nicki prepares new copywriters for the inevitable panic that follows landing their first client, with practical reframes and a structured writing process.

## Key takeaways
- The pattern is universal: elation then panic (about 3 seconds apart) when the first client says yes
- Nervousness is natural — the brain tries to keep you in your comfort zone; reframe fear as excitement
- Fastest cure for panic: sit down and get to work immediately
- Never skip concepting phase — going straight to writing produces weaker copy
- Process: creative brief → concepting → key messages outline → headlines/subheads → first draft → sleep → second draft → polish
- You won't get it perfect the first time; that's why revisions exist; deadlines are your friend

## Notable claims
- "Your creative brief is your roadmap." *(article)*
- Deadlines move you from "thinking about writing" to "sitting at your desk writing" *(article)*
