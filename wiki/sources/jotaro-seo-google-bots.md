# Les Google Bots — Summary

**One-liner:** Foundational explainer on Googlebot crawlers — how they work, why they matter for SEO, how crawl frequency and crawl budget determine which pages get indexed.

## Key takeaways
- Googlebots (also called Spiders/Crawlers) explore the web by following links page-to-page, analyzing text, links, tags, images to categorize and index content.
- Good **[[maillage-interne]]** (internal linking) is explicitly cited as crucial for bots to navigate effectively.
- Google's index is a massive database; appearing in it is a prerequisite for any SERP visibility.
- **Crawl frequency** = determined by site relevance and content update frequency. Regular publishing = more frequent crawling.
- **[[crawl-budget]]** = the time/resources Google allocates to exploring a site. Must be managed to ensure priority pages are crawled.
- Bots determine site category by analyzing keywords and content — reinforces importance of semantic consistency ([[topical-authority]]).
