# NavBoost — Summary

**One-liner:** NavBoost is Google's ranking system that uses real user-behavior signals (CTR, pogo-sticking, dwell time, implicit satisfaction) as a refinement layer on top of classic factors — putting the user, not just technique, at the center of ranking.

## Key takeaways
- **NavBoost = behavior-based dynamic ranking adjustment.** Surfaced in Google internal docs/technical reports (DOJ-era leaks). It observes what users do *after* seeing results and raises/lowers pages accordingly.
- **Core signals:** **CTR** (clicks vs other results), **pogo-sticking** (immediate return to SERP = dissatisfaction), **dwell time** (time on page), internal engagement (scroll, link clicks, media), **implicit satisfaction** (not returning to Google = found the answer).
- **Not a replacement** for backlinks/domain authority — an **extra refinement layer**.
- **NavDemotion = the inverse:** negative signals demote pages — high pogo-sticking, very low dwell time, below-average CTR, duplicate/thin content.
- **Query types affected:** all, but strongest where behavior validates relevance — informational (dwell/engagement), navigational (CTR on the right result), transactional (quick exit = unmet intent). Acts as a **quality filter**.

## Adaptation strategies (with locations)
- **Content for engagement** *(§Optimiser le contenu)*: strong intros, fast-readability structure (short paragraphs, subheads, lists, bold), straight to the point, examples/data/storytelling for retention.
- **UX** *(§UX)*: speed (slow → pogo-sticking), mobile-first, clear/accessible design, avoid intrusive ads/popups.
- **Track data** *(§Suivre)*: GA4 (engagement), GSC (CTR per query), heatmaps/session recordings (Hotjar, Clarity).
