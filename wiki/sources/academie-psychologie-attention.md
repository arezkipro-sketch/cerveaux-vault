# Psychologie de l'attention — Summary

**One-liner:** The reader decides in ~3 seconds whether to continue; attention is limited and selective, so the message must be clear, distinct and instantly relevant.

## Key takeaways
- **Attention is filtered:** the brain screens stimuli; copy must be clear, distinct, immediately relevant.
- **Title + opener** carry the first impression — catchy + specific.
- **3-second rule:** after the title, the first sentences decide; open on a striking stat or intriguing question, lead with a concrete benefit, avoid vague intros.
- **Prolong attention with biases:** curiosity (tease), scarcity, social proof.
- **Visual structure guides the eye:** short paragraphs, clear subheads, bullet lists, bold key terms. Dense text repels.
