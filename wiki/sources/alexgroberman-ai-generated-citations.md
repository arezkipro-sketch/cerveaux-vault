# ~1 in 6 AI-Cited Sources Is AI-Generated — Summary

**One-liner:** Study of 19,154 sources cited by ChatGPT/Copilot/Gemini/Perplexity found ~16 % show evidence of AI-generated content — meaning AI systems are increasingly citing AI-generated sources, but what matters for citation is having unique, verifiable, non-generic information.

## Key takeaways
- Study: 712 real-world questions (policy, health, environment) submitted to ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, Perplexity → 2,848 responses → 26,266 unique URLs → 19,154 successfully analyzed → **~16 % flagged as likely AI-generated** → [[ai-seo]].
- **By platform** (likely AI-generated rates): Copilot **27.8 %**, Gemini **14.7 %**, Perplexity **9.4 %**, ChatGPT **7.3 %**.
- **Key finding**: AI content CAN be cited by AI — AI-assisting writing doesn't block citation. What matters is content quality and uniqueness.
- **Citation concentration**: top 25 domains = 23.8 % of all citations. 59.1 % of domains cited only once. 16.5 % cited twice. Long tail of small sites also gets cited.
- **What differentiates cited vs. uncited content**: unique original data, specific verifiable facts, statistics, pricing, customer results, expert explanations, useful comparisons, transparent sources, current information.
- Generic content = low citation probability; proprietary data = high citation probability.

## Notable claims
- "AI systems are literally searching for AI answers to answer human questions" — author's framing.
- "You don't need to become Wikipedia — you need to become the clearest, most useful source for one specific part of the customer's question" → [[ai-seo]].
- Citation concentration is high but the long tail is large — small sites CAN earn citations → [[ai-seo]].
