# Pinterest Ads — Summary

**One-liner:** Pinterest Ads is inspiration-based advertising — it reaches users *early* in the buying journey (planning/comparing, not being interrupted), with long-lived pins, intention-based targeting, and strong ROAS for considered/visual purchases. *(Raw filename mistitled "interest Ads".)*

## Key takeaways
- **Pinterest = discovery/intention engine**, not distraction: users come to plan, compare, decide. Google captures hot intent, Facebook interrupts, **Pinterest accompanies active reflection** — "advertising based on inspiration, not urgency."
- **Ads = sponsored pins**, native in feed/search/boards. Formats: sponsored pins (image/video), carousel, **Idea Pins**, **product collections**, **shopping/catalog ads**.
- **Differentiators:** ads feel like useful suggestions; **long content lifespan** (a pin drives traffic for months vs a Meta ad dying in ~48h); it's a visual search engine.
- **Targeting by intention** (> demographics): keywords, interests, behavior, lookalike audiences, retargeting (site visitors, pin/video engagers).
- **ROAS:** strong in decor, e-commerce, tourism, fashion, beauty, real estate, education; lower CPC than FB/IG; longer visibility; more qualified traffic; better brand recall.

## Strategy notes (with locations)
- **Not one-shot/impulse** — nurtures reflection → preference → final selection (upper/mid funnel anticipation) *(§Comparatif)*.
- **Winning targeting combos:** keywords+interests, lookalike+retargeting, continuous creative tests *(§public cible)*.
- **Creative:** vertical readable images, moderate on-image text, **before/after**, real (not catalog) situations, constant A/B testing — "your ad should look like an idea, not an ad" *(§Optimisation des créatifs)*.
- **Worth it?** Yes for reflection/comparison/visual-inspiration offers; no for impulse/urgency products *(§en vaut-il la peine)*.
