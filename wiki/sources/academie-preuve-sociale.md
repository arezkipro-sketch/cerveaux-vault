# Preuve sociale — Summary

**One-liner:** Social proof answers "why should I believe you?" by showing others already got results — lowers perceived risk, raises credibility; place it to kill a specific objection.

## Key takeaways
- **Why it works:** under uncertainty, humans follow others. Reviews influence ~90% of purchase decisions; best-sellers sell faster; third-party labels build trust.
- **6 forms:** (1) testimonials/reviews (precise, credible w/ name+role+photo, varied) (2) case studies + before/after (3) numbers/stats (verifiable, current) (4) labels/certifications/media (Qualiopi, awards) (5) community/social counts (6) user-generated proof (screenshots of DMs, stories, tweets — with consent).
- **Where to place:** sales/landing pages (after the offer + before final CTA), about page, email sequences, blog posts, social.
- **Best practice:** tell a story not "C'était super!", stay authentic (real words), vary formats, give context, refresh, get consent (RGPD), tie each proof to a benefit.
- **Mistakes:** generic/fake-sounding testimonials, copied proof, overloading (too much proof kills proof), ignoring objections, missing CTA after proof.
