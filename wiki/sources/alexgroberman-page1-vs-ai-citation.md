# Page 1 Rank ≠ AI Citation — Summary

**One-liner:** Two studies confirm that Google AI Overviews and Gemini operate on different source logic than traditional Search: ~30 % of AI Overview-cited domains didn't appear in the first-page organic results shown alongside them.

## Key takeaways
- Study of **55,393 Google searches**: ~30 % of domains cited inside AI Overviews did NOT appear in first-page organic results alongside them → [[ai-overviews]].
- Separate study of **11,500 queries**: minimal source overlap between traditional Google Search, AI Overviews, and Gemini → [[ai-overviews]].
- **Google AI doesn't just copy the first page**: it runs related searches, investigates subtopics, selects a different group of supporting pages.
- **AI visibility = 2 layers**:
  1. Traditional SEO eligibility + authority (still required as baseline).
  2. Having specific, useful content that directly supports the answer Google is trying to generate.
- A smaller company with detailed pages on construction payroll, multi-state compliance, pricing may be cited over a big brand with a generic "payroll software" page → [[ai-seo]].
- **Biggest mistake**: assuming page-one rankings automatically produce AI visibility.

## What triggers AI citation (from author's interpretation)
- Clear product/service descriptions.
- Audience-specific use cases.
- Pricing.
- Original statistics.
- Detailed comparisons.
- Customer results.
- Industry-specific guidance.
- Evidence supporting claims.
