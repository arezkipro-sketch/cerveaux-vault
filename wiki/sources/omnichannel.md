# Omnichannel — Summary

**One-liner:** Omnichannel integrates all of a brand's channels (physical + digital) into one seamless, customer-centered journey — distinct from multichannel (present everywhere but disconnected) and single channel (one channel only).

## Key takeaways
- **Omnichannel = total channel integration** for a unified experience: the customer moves between channels without repeating info; history, preferences, and interests follow them. The in-store seller knows what was viewed online.
- **3 pillars:** (1) **data integration** (real-time shared data, unified DB), (2) **channel connection** (channels talk to each other; start on web, finish in app/store), (3) **customer-experience priority** (the customer, not the channel, at the center).
- **Omnichannel vs multichannel vs single channel:**
  - *Omnichannel* — highest integration; interconnected, seamless.
  - *Multichannel* — present on many channels but **not connected** (re-explain each time → friction).
  - *Single channel* — one channel only; limited reach/flexibility = competitive disadvantage.
- **Enablers:** CRM, marketing automation, AI, data analysis.
- **Benefits:** better CX, higher conversion, loyalty/retention, centralized data, competitive advantage.

## How to build it (with locations)
1. Map the customer journey → 2. Integrate systems/data (CRM, e-commerce, ERP, marketing) → 3. Personalize → 4. Unify customer service → 5. Train teams + align processes → 6. Monitor + optimize continuously *(§Comment développer)*.

## Notable claims (with locations)
- **70%+ of consumers use multiple channels** in their purchase journey — HBR study of 46,000 shoppers *(§Qu'est-ce que)*.

## Future trends
- AI (predictive analytics, hyper-personalization), **phygital** experiences, AR/VR (retail/fashion), voice assistants/advanced chatbots, privacy/data governance.
