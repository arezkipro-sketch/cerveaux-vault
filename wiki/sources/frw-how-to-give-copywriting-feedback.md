# How to Give Copywriting Feedback — Summary

**One-liner:** Practical framework for giving and receiving feedback in copywriting relationships — both to clients and to junior writers you manage.

## Key takeaways
- Feedback is a skill that must be practiced; copywriters should approach clients as partners with positive intent
- Before any project, establish how you best receive feedback (video call, inline comments, consolidated email)
- Client feedback tips: be timely, choose the right medium, give the "why and benefit," schedule regular check-ins
- For junior writers: show them the "what" not the "how" — explain why something is off, don't rewrite it for them
- Positive framing: do a "Control F" for negative contractions (don't, can't, won't) and reframe positively before sending feedback
- Hiring junior copywriters is one of the best ways to scale to six-figure and seven-figure income

## Notable claims
- Clients don't know how to be clients — it's the copywriter's job to guide them *(article)*
- "Show them the what not the how" — same principle as pitching: tell clients what you can do, not how *(article)*
