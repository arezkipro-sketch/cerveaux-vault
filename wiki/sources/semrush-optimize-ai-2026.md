# Optimize Content for AI Search 2026 — Summary

**One-liner:** Semrush 7-step checklist for AI search optimization — focuses on content structure signals (H2/H3 as questions, 40-60 word direct answers, definition → detail → example pattern), E-E-A-T signals for LLMs, and semantic keyword cooccurrence as a new AI ranking signal.

## Key takeaways

### AI citation signals (what AI models look for)
- Credible named authors with relevant qualifications → [[e-e-a-t]].
- Original content: first-hand data, in-house analysis, expert commentary.
- Clean structure: semantic HTML, appropriate headings, organized layout.
- Freshness: recently published/updated (especially in fast-moving sectors like AI, finance).
- Domain trust: strong backlink profile + mentions on respected sites → [[seo-audit]].

### Google AI Overviews specifically
- Schema markup may improve clarity (debated).
- **Fresh content** can be a tiebreaker in competitive spaces.
- **Top-ranked pages are cited more** (Benford's prominence law): pages that rank higher organically get cited disproportionately in AI summaries → good SEO still helps.
- **Keyword cooccurrence**: brand appearing near relevant topic terms repeatedly → AI associates brand with topic.

### Semantic keyword cooccurrence (new concept)
- LLMs rely on cooccurrence patterns from training data: terms that frequently appear together.
- When your brand consistently appears near target keywords (across multiple sources), AI models associate them.
- Example: "Monday.com" + "workflow automation" appearing repeatedly across articles → AI cites Monday.com for workflow automation queries → [[ai-seo]].
- Implication: brand PR and mentions in topic-relevant contexts matter for AI visibility.

### AI content optimization checklist
| Tactic | Why it helps |
|---|---|
| Target question-based queries (how to, what is, best way) | Trigger AI Overviews and LLM responses |
| Structure H2/H3 as questions | AI maps search intent to page structure |
| Answer directly and concisely (40-60 words) | Respects snippet format, improves citation chance |
| Use bullet points and numbered lists | Matches LLM output format |
| Use definition → detail → example pattern | Predictable structure → easier for AI to parse |
| Include original data / stats | +61 % absorption lift (per [[alexgroberman-citation-absorption]]) |
| Named expert authors | E-E-A-T credibility signal |

### What changed since 2023
- AI models now multimodal (Gemini, Claude 3, GPT-4o): process images + voice.
- Content is extracted as fragments and recombined → each section must be independently comprehensible (no "as mentioned above" references that collapse without context).

## Connections
- Operationalizes [[citation-absorption]]: checklist maps directly to the content type lifts (stats, definitions, how-to).
- Semantic cooccurrence = new mechanism beyond absorption/selection → updates [[ai-seo]].
- "Section must be independently comprehensible" principle aligns with [[article-structure]] heading rules.
- "Top-ranked pages cited more" partially contradicts [[alexgroberman-62pct-beyond-top10]] (62 % of AI citations outside top-10). Resolution: for Google AI Overviews specifically, rank still matters; for ChatGPT, less so.
