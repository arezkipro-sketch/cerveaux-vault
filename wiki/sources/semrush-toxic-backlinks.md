# Backlinks toxiques — Summary

**One-liner:** Toxic backlinks are inbound links that violate Google's link-spam guidelines and can hurt rankings; mostly devalued, but volume can trigger a manual action/penalty.

## Key takeaways
- **Definition:** links placed for SEO manipulation, not user benefit. Google devalues them; systematic spam → manual action (de-ranking/removal).
- **9 causes:** paying for follow links, link exchanges (excess reciprocal), PBNs, link bots, unnatural links on forums/comments, low-quality directories, widget links, contract-mandated links, + (9th in body).
- **Qualify paid/UGC links:** `rel="nofollow"` (ignore for ranking), `rel="sponsored"` (paid), `rel="ugc"` (user-generated) — use for ads, gifted products, influencers, paid directories, widgets.
- **Remediate:** assess with a toxicity score (Semrush Backlink Audit), request removal, or disavow.
