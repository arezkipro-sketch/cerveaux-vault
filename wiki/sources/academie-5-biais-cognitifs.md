# 5 biais cognitifs — Summary

**One-liner:** Five mental shortcuts (a Cialdini-style set) the brain uses to decide fast; using them makes copy more persuasive — framed as understanding, not manipulation.

## Key takeaways
- **Scarcity** (peur de manquer): rarer = higher perceived value. Limited quantity ("Plus que 3 places"), clear deadline, early-bird bonuses.
- **Authority:** we trust perceived experts. Show concrete results, client testimonials, cite recognised sources (e.g. Kahneman).
- **Reciprocity:** a free gift creates an unconscious urge to give back. Free guide, sample, practical tips before the sale.
- **Social proof:** people validate decisions by others' behaviour. Authentic testimonials, user/student counts, detailed case studies.
- **Commitment & consistency:** people stay consistent with past choices. Ask a small initial commitment (newsletter signup) → logical progression (free guide → full course).
- **Stance:** "les biais cognitifs ne sont pas de la manipulation" — they reflect how the brain works; use ethically.
