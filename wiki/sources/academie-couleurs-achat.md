# Couleurs et achat — Summary

**One-liner:** Colour is a psychological stimulus, not just aesthetics — up to ~90% of first impression is colour-linked; choose by emotion, contrast for CTAs, consistency across channels.

## Key takeaways
- **Colour meanings:** red = urgency/energy (CTAs), blue = trust/pro (B2B/finance), green = growth/health/nature, yellow = optimism (accent), purple = luxury/creativity, orange = friendly decision-nudge (buttons), black = elegance/premium, white = simplicity/breathing room. Meanings vary by culture.
- **4 principles:** align with brand identity; visual hierarchy (primary + secondary + contrast colour for CTAs); accessibility/contrast; multichannel consistency.
- **CTAs:** contrasting colour so the button stands out, same colour for all primary CTAs (build a reflex), pair with punchy micro-copy.
- **Mistakes:** too many colours (limit 2-3), ignoring culture, copying competitors, not testing.
