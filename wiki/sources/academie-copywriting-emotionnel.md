# Copywriting émotionnel — Summary

**One-liner:** Not "writing with emotion" but writing to trigger a *useful* emotion, then guiding to a calm logical decision — "l'émotion ouvre la porte, la preuve et la clarté la referment".

## Key takeaways
- **Core sequence:** **Émotion → Clarté → Preuve → Action.** Emotion drives attention, memory, action; the rational part then verifies (credible? fit? risky? now?).
- **3-move definition:** reflect the reader's lived experience (felt understood) → activate one dominant emotion → add structure + proof (decide).
- **6 emotional levers (without forcing):** healthy fear (real risk of inaction), desire (freedom/recognition/security/pride/mastery), relief, belonging, pride/identity, curiosity (easiest entry).
- **Message architecture:** (1) hook on lived experience (2) measured agitation — precision over drama (3) sensory, simple solution projection (4) proof + risk reduction (5) one obvious CTA.
- **6-step method:** pick ONE dominant emotion → "vous" + precise hook → stage a micro-scene → describe the concrete "after" → seed micro-proofs throughout → end on one simple next step.
- **Language:** action verbs, short sentences, 1-2 line paragraphs, light sensory images, zero jargon, write to one person.
- **Mistakes:** vague promise, over-dramatising, stacked bonuses, no proof, multiple CTAs, "marketing" tone.
