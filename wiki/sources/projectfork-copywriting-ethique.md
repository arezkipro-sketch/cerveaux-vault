# Copywriting éthique (e-commerce) — Summary

**One-liner:** Ethical copy — trust + transparency over manipulation — drives *durable* conversion; manipulation gives a one-off spike but high reputation risk.

## Key takeaways
- **Ethical vs manipulative (table):** ethical = high trust, stable/growing conversion, low risk; manipulative = low trust, momentary spike, high reputation risk. Brands that avoid over-promising retain better.
- **Principles:** concrete benefits + proof, reassuring microcopy on critical actions, no vague/deceptive claims, clear verifiable CTAs.
- **Across the journey:** product pages (clear benefits, real dimensions, guarantees, verified reviews), checkout (reassuring microcopy on delivery/security/returns → fewer abandons, per Baymard), post-purchase transparency.
- **Measure:** A/B tests + KPIs (conversion rate, cart abandon, 30-day retention, AOV); track post-purchase cancellations, not just sales.
