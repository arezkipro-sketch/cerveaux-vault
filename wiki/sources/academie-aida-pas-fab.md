# AIDA, PAS, FAB — Summary

**One-liner:** The three core copywriting structures and when to use each: AIDA for long sales pages/ads, PAS for short punchy messages, FAB for product sheets.

## Key takeaways
- **AIDA** = Attention (punchy headline/hook) → Interest (name a familiar problem) → Desire (show solution fits need) → Action (clear CTA). Best for sales pages + long ads.
- **PAS** = Problème → Agitation (stress the consequences) → Solution. Best for emails, ads, short sales copy.
- **FAB** = Features (technical traits) → Advantages (what they do) → Benefits (how life concretely improves). Best for product sheets + commercial pitches.
- **Why they work:** they align the text with the reader's psychological journey — attention → interest → desire → action.

## Notable claims (with locations)
- AIDA example: sales page opens on a striking number (Attention), explains why most fail alone (Interest), shows how the training solves it (Desire), ends on immediate signup (Action). — *(§AIDA)*
- PAS email opener: "Vous perdez du temps à écrire sans résultats?" — *(§PAS)*
