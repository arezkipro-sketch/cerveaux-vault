# FRW Ep. 283: Pricing Guides Are Garbage — Summary

**One-liner:** Nicki and Kate argue that copywriting pricing guides are misleading marketing opt-ins that can cause freelancers to accidentally work for minimum wage.

## Key takeaways
- Copywriting is not a commodity — price depends on project scope, client organization, expertise required, UX strategy involvement, number of revisions
- Pricing guides are "marketing opt-ins" preying on writers' fear and desire for easy answers
- Risk of following flat-rate guides: a "simple" $5,000 website can become minimum-wage work if scope explodes
- Right approach: determine target hourly rate, then map every phase (discovery call → revisions → creative reviews) to derive project price
- You have more information than any guide — use your own calculations based on your goals and how you work
- Stop being "psychic" about client budget — price based on your own reality

## Notable claims
- "Copywriting is not a banana." — Kate *(episode)*
- "These guides are a great opt-in promotion because they're catchy and people want an easy answer, but they are playing on your fears." — Nicki *(episode)*
- "You have more information about the project, how you work, and what you want to make than any guide ever could." — Nicki *(episode)*
