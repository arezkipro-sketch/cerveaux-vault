# IA x SEO — Summary

**One-liner:** Practical guide to getting cited by ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini — based on Ahrefs' analysis of 1 billion+ data points across 14 studies.

## Key takeaways
- Google SEO and AI citation are two distinct games: only **13.7 % overlap** in sources cited by Google AI Mode vs. AI Overviews.
- **28.3 % of pages most cited by ChatGPT have zero organic Google visibility** — ranking #1 on Google does not protect you.
- The single highest-correlated factor with AI brand visibility is **YouTube presence** (correlation = **0.737**), above all classic SEO signals.
- **"Best X" list pages = 43.8 % of all ChatGPT citations** — the most citable content format by far.
- **99.9 % of AI Overviews appear on informational queries** (only 3.2 % transactional) — content strategy must skew heavily educational.
- AI crawlers visit dozens of pages per query but only cite ~50 % of them — being crawled ≠ being cited.

## Notable claims (with locations)
- AI Overviews reduce clicks to the #1 organic result by **58 %** (was 34.5 % ten months ago) — *(intro)*.
- **67 % of top 1,000 ChatGPT citations** come from uncontrollable sources: Wikipedia (29.7 %), homepages (23.8 %), app stores (6.6 %) — *(Constat 4)*.
- Ahrefs study: 1 billion+ data points, 14 studies — *(intro)*.
- Google AI Mode and AI Overviews agree on what to say 86 % of the time but cite different sources (13.7 % overlap) — *(Erreur 1)*.
- LLM.txt = robots.txt equivalent for AI crawlers; low adoption, 30-minute implementation — *(Stratégie 5)*.
- Optimal content mix for AI visibility: 70 % informational, 20 % commercial-hybrid, 10 % transactional — *(Stratégie 6)*.

## Connections / contradictions
- Reinforces [[cocon-semantique]]: topical depth coverage is the AI citation strategy too (but broader than internal link architecture).
- New angle on [[link-juice]]: AI "trust" is now also built through external platform mentions (YouTube, forums, podcasts) — a broader form of link equity.
- Partially contradicts the conventional SEO wisdom that Google ranking = visibility: 28.3 % of top ChatGPT-cited pages rank nowhere on Google.

## Open questions
- Does LLM.txt actually improve citation rates? (author himself skeptical, calls it "un peu bullshit")
- How quickly do AI models update their citation preferences after new content is published?
- Does YouTube presence cause AI visibility, or do both reflect underlying brand authority?
