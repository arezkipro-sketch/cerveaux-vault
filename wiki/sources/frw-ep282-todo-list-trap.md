# FRW Ep. 282: The To-Do List Trap — Summary

**One-liner:** Nicki and Kate dismantle the "to-do list trap" — the compulsion to complete every written task — and offer a focused, deletable-task framework for copywriters.

## Key takeaways
- The trap: feeling you must do everything on your list because you wrote it down
- Solution: limit daily list to 1–2 items; push everything else to a "parking lot"
- If a task stays red for 2 days, the project is too large — break it into smaller steps
- "Fake accomplishments" (clearing wishlists, watching old coaching calls) are "Resistance" in disguise, displacing high-impact work
- Empowering realization: deleting a task you never did can feel as good as completing it
- AI as "external brain": use ChatGPT to categorize and prioritize chaotic ideas rather than as a writing replacement

## Notable claims
- "I use AI as an external brain… to help me make sense of all this chaos." — Nicki *(episode)*
- Asana "schedule next step" workflow prevents cascade reschedules when life interrupts *(episode)*
- Decision fatigue from long lists makes creative work harder *(episode)*
