# Effet miroir — Summary

**One-liner:** Reflect the audience's own words, emotions and story back to them ("c'est moi") — vendre sans forcer by being *accurate*, not pushy.

## Key takeaways
- **What:** write in a way that resembles the prospect's inner voice — by observing then rephrasing precisely, never inventing. Does 3 things: shows you understand the lived problem, reduces mental effort, raises emotional impact.
- **3 levels:** (1) lexical mirror — reuse exact recurring words ("je stagne", "je tourne en rond") (2) emotional mirror — phrase the real feeling (3) narrative mirror — tell the prospect's typical journey (problem→attempts→doubt→click→solution→transformation).
- **Sources of real words:** comments (YouTube/LinkedIn/Reddit/forums/groups), client calls (goldmine — note exact phrases), recurring questions.
- **Method:** collect 30 real phrases (frustrations/desires/objections/situations/emotional words) → pick 1 person + 1 situation → write a 3-line intro (problem mirror → feeling mirror → clarity promise) → add proof of understanding → make the CTA a mirror too.
- **4 mistakes:** copy-pasting too literally, projecting assumptions, overdoing it (sounds manipulative), sacrificing fluidity.
