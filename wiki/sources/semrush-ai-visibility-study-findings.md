# AI Visibility Study — Community Content Beats Brand — Summary

**One-liner:** Semrush study across 5 sectors (finance, tech, business services, consumer electronics, fashion) using ChatGPT + Google AI Mode: community-edited sources (Wikipedia, Reddit) dominate AI citations — even in regulated YMYL sectors — while branded marketing content rarely appears. 4 surprising findings.

## Key takeaways

### Finding 1 — Community content > brand marketing in AI citations
- **Wikipedia** is the #1 or #2 source in **4 of 5 sectors** studied → [[ai-seo]].
- Wikipedia digital tech citation frequency: **167 %** in ChatGPT responses (cited more than once per query on average).
- Review platforms like G2 rank 4th in tech citations (20 % ChatGPT frequency). Brand websites rarely appear.
- Apple's own marketing pages can't compete with Wikipedia's neutral product specs when AI needs authoritative info.
- **Why**: AI models trust "collective wisdom" over polished marketing — community sources are expected to be objective and factual → [[e-e-a-t]].

### Finding 2 — Reddit dominates even YMYL finance queries
- **Reddit cited in 176 %** of ChatGPT financial queries — nearly 2× per query on average → [[ai-seo]].
- Context: Finance is a YMYL (Your Money Your Life) category where Google traditionally requires expert authority.
- Google AI Mode does NOT show this behavior — it prefers Bankrate (86 %) and NerdWallet (75 %) for finance.
- **Cross-sector Reddit dominance in ChatGPT**: business services 141 %, tech 121 %, consumer electronics 127 %.
- Authentic community engagement outperforms expert PR campaigns for ChatGPT AI citation.

### Finding 3 — Branded sites get few direct citations
- Traditional content marketing → brand-owned blog posts → not what AI cites.
- AI models prefer third-party neutral sources that validate claims → mentions ON community sources = higher citation.
- Implication: invest in being mentioned/validated IN community sources, not just publishing on your own site.

### Finding 4 — ChatGPT and Google AI Mode behave very differently
- ChatGPT favors Reddit, Wikipedia, community sources.
- Google AI Mode favors established authority sites (NerdWallet, Bankrate, category leaders).
- Different algorithms, different optimization strategies needed for each platform → [[citation-absorption]].

## Strategic implications
- Update Wikipedia entries with accurate, verifiable technical information → high ROI.
- Participate authentically in Reddit communities relevant to your sector (G2 reviews, Quora, forums).
- Replace promotional brand language with factual, neutral, precise specifications.
- Monitor which community sources AI cites for your category → engage there.

## Connections / contradictions
- **Contradicts E-E-A-T as traditionally applied**: E-E-A-T emphasizes expert named authors and branded expertise. But AI cites Reddit (no single expert author) over expert sites in YMYL → [[e-e-a-t]] needs nuance.
- Extends [[citation-absorption]]: community content doesn't just get selected — it gets absorbed more deeply.
- Complements [[semrush-ai-visibility-trends-3mo]] (time series of same platform behavior data).
- Surprising given [[jotaro-seo-chatgpt-sales]] framing (which implies brand authority via E-E-A-T → AI citations). That applies more to Google AI Mode than ChatGPT.
