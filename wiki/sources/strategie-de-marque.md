# Stratégie de marque — Summary

**One-liner:** A brand strategy is the structured plan for how a company wants to be perceived (purpose, values, mission, identity, tone, positioning) — far beyond a logo; this guide covers why it matters, the 4 brand-architecture types, and a 5-step build.

## Key takeaways
- **Brand strategy = guidelines for perception:** raison d'être, values, mission, visual identity, tone, market positioning — shaping the whole experience at every touchpoint, aligned to business objectives.
- **Why it matters:** differentiate on **value not price**, coherence/credibility, consumer trust, attract talent/partners. "A brand without a strategy is just one more brand."

## The 4 brand-architecture types (with examples)
- **Product brand** — independent identity per product; house invisible (P&G: Gillette, Ariel, Pantene). Reaches diverse segments without cannibalizing.
- **Range/extension brand ("par portée")** — decline the main brand for segments, keep the core (Coca-Cola Zero/Light/Sans Sucres).
- **Umbrella brand ("ombrelle")** — one brand carries many products; transfers trust (Apple: iPhone/iPad/MacBook).
- **Endorsement brand ("caution")** — parent endorses sub-brands with own identity (Nestlé → Nescafé, Nesquik; "by Nestlé").

## 5-step build (with locations)
1. Define **mission / vision / values** (the foundation) → 2. Analyze **market + competition** (positioning) → 3. Build **brand identity** (visual + verbal: name/logo/colors/typo/tone) → 4. Define **content strategy + digital marketing** → 5. Set up a **brand agency / dedicated team** *(§Comment créer)*.

## Notable examples
- Apple (logo = innovation/exclusivity), Nike (performance/self-transcendence, emotional), Vendée Globe (regional/territory brand).
