# 62 % of AI Overview Citations Beyond Top 10 — Summary

**One-liner:** Ahrefs study (863K SERPs, 4M AI Overview URLs): only 38 % of AI Overview citations now come from top-10 organic pages — down from 76 % a year ago — because Google Gemini 3 decomposes queries into sub-queries, each drawing from its own source pool.

## Key takeaways
- **62 %** of AI Overview citations go to pages NOT in the top 10 organic results for the original query → [[ai-overviews]].
- One year ago, 76 % came from top 10. Now only 38 %. The shift accelerated after Google upgraded to Gemini 3 → [[ai-overviews]].
- **31 %** of all AI Overview citations come from pages ranking beyond position 100 for the original query → [[ai-overviews]].
- **Root cause — sub-query decomposition**: Google AI internally generates sub-queries (e.g., "CRM onboarding time comparison 2026", "CRM pricing for teams under 10") for each search, then draws sources from each sub-query independently → [[ai-seo]].
- **Implication**: a business ranking #3 for its main keyword only captures 38 % of AI citation opportunities. The remaining 62 % go to whoever covered the sub-questions.

## Strategic implications
- **Content depth over keyword coverage**: 40-60 pages covering every sub-question a buyer asks (comparisons, specs, use cases, integrations, pricing) = access to the 62 %.
- **Domain authority required**: sub-query ranking still requires sufficient domain authority — backlinks that lift the entire domain, not just one page.
- **Category coverage mindset**: stop thinking "which keywords should I rank for?" → "which questions does any buyer in my category ask?" → [[topical-authority]].

## Notable data (with source)
- Ahrefs study: 863,000 SERPs, 4 million AI Overview URLs, Feb 2026 → [[ai-overviews]].
- 31 % of citations from pages beyond position 100 for the original query.
- Google AI internally decomposes: "best CRM for small business" → sub-queries: "CRM onboarding comparison", "CRM pricing <10 people", "multi-state CRM compliance".
