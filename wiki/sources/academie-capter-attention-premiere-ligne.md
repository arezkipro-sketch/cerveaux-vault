# Capter l'attention dès la première ligne — Summary

**One-liner:** The title earns the click; the first line decides whether the reader stays — its only job is to make them want to read the second line.

## Key takeaways
- **The first line must produce ≥1 of:** "I feel concerned", curiosity, tension, a visible benefit, trust. Otherwise the reader leaves.
- **Its 4 jobs:** confirm the title's promise, create connection ("they get me"), give a reason to read on, set tone + credibility.
- **Golden rule:** one line = one mission — *retain*, not explain/contextualise/summarise.
- **11 attention techniques:** (1) speak directly "vous" (2) punchy question (3) open a curiosity loop (4) contradict a received idea (5) clear concrete promise (6) describe a precise symptom (identification) (7) blunt truth (8) micro-story (9) state the stakes/cost of inaction (10) before/after contrast (11) mini-test/interaction.
- **Opening-paragraph model:** Hook → Symptom → Consequence → Promise → Transition.
- **Mistakes:** empty phrases ("Bienvenue dans cet article…", "Aujourd'hui nous allons voir…"), too formal/distant, over-promising w/o proof, lack of clarity.
