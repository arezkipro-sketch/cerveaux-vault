# Copywriting : définition et exemples concrets — Summary

**One-liner:** Foundational definition piece — copywriting is persuasive writing that drives the reader to act, focused on conversion and emotional connection, distinct from plain content writing.

## Key takeaways
- **Definition:** copywriting = the art of writing persuasive text that pushes the reader to act (buy, subscribe, download a guide, request a quote). Centred on **conversion** + an emotional link, unlike ordinary content writing.
- **Why it matters:** in a message-saturated market, good copy lets you stand out — grab attention fast, spark interest with a targeted message, build emotional connection, drive action. (Mirrors the AIDA arc.)
- **Three example formats:** sales page (problem → solution → concrete benefits), follow-up email (abandoned-cart reminder + limited offer), social ad (hook question: *"Vous voulez doubler vos ventes sans dépenser plus en publicité ?"*).
- **Four elements of good copy:** a punchy headline, precise audience understanding, clear benefits over mere features, a direct motivating call-to-action.
- **How to learn:** study persuasion techniques, analyse high-performing examples, practise regularly. (Article ends as a promo for L'Académie™ paid training.)
