# How to Use AI the Right Way as a Writer (2026) — Summary

**One-liner:** Alex Cattoni's 3-step framework for using AI without losing creative integrity: Audit Workflow → Train AI like a team member → Keep Your Humanity.

## Key takeaways
- Both pessimistic (AI replaces all) and optimistic (AI saves everything) views are harmful — both assume humans have no agency
- AI is a tool, not a takeover. Creativity is a muscle — outsourcing every thought atrophies it.
- Refusing to use AI in 2026 is not protecting creativity — it's leaving leverage on the table
- 3-step framework: (1) Audit your workflow — granularize tasks, identify which require your voice; (2) Train AI like a team member — give brand voice, writing samples, rules; (3) Keep your humanity — your story, opinions, lived experience are your #1 competitive advantage
- Giant tech companies (Anthropic, OpenAI) are actively hiring human storytellers right now (WSJ)
- AI content has officially surpassed human content online in volume — making authentic human voice scarcer and more valuable
- Ethical rule: "Would I be proud to put my name on this?" — apply before publishing anything AI-assisted

## Notable claims
- "AI is a TOOL. Not a takeover." — Alex Cattoni *(article)*
- "In a world full of AI-generated everything, the most rebellious thing you can do is stay deeply, unapologetically human." *(article)*
- Vague prompts → generic output; well-trained context-rich AI assistant → far better output *(article)*
- Personal incident: a marketer used AI to generate an image of a woman that looked like Alex to promote his tool without consent — ethical red flag story *(article)*
