# Citation Selection vs Citation Absorption — Summary

**One-liner:** Study of 21,143 AI citations distinguishes two levels of AI visibility: being listed as a source (citation selection) vs. actually shaping what the AI says (citation absorption) — and the signals that drive each are different.

## Key takeaways

### The two-level framework
- **Citation selection**: AI platform discovers your page and includes it in sources attached to a response. Easy to detect; doesn't guarantee influence → [[citation-absorption]].
- **Citation absorption**: how much of your page's information actually shapes the generated response — the AI repeats your facts, uses your comparisons, adopts your positioning → [[citation-absorption]].
- A business cited 8th may contribute nothing to the answer. A business cited once may have its pricing, comparisons, and use cases fully integrated → [[citation-absorption]].

### Platform data (602 prompts, 21,143 citations)
- Average sources cited per prompt: ChatGPT 6.88, Google 12.06, Perplexity 16.35.
- Average influence score per cited page: **ChatGPT 0.2713**, Perplexity 0.0646, Google 0.0584.
- ChatGPT cites fewer sources but uses them more deeply → [[citation-absorption]].

### High-influence page characteristics
- High-influence pages (top quartile): avg ~1,943 words, 10.59 headings, 47.49 paragraphs.
- Low-influence pages (bottom quartile): avg ~170 words, 0.85 headings, 8.34 paragraphs.
- Content types with higher average influence (vs. pages without):
  - Code: **+76.88 %**
  - Numbers/statistics: **+61.55 %**
  - Definitions: **+57.33 %**
  - Comparisons: **+55.28 %**
  - How-to/practical content: **+41.20 %**

### Key nuance
- Length helps when combined with: useful structure, semantic alignment, evidence density, multiple extractable facts.
- A long page of vague marketing language still has near-zero influence.
- Q&A formatting NOT associated with higher absorption (slightly lower, though causation not established).

## Notable claims
- "Being cited ≠ influencing the AI answer" — the main insight of the paper → [[citation-absorption]].
- Practical implication: optimize for absorption, not just citation count.

## Connections / contradictions
- Extends [[jotaro-seo-ia-x-seo]]: factual density rule now quantified (stats +61.55 %, definitions +57.33 %).
- Extends [[article-structure]]: high heading count + paragraph density correlates with absorption.
- Adds nuance to [[ai-seo]]: distinction between being selected vs. being influential — brands should optimize for the latter.
- Contradicts Q&A formatting assumption: FAQ structure does NOT correlate with higher absorption (interesting tension with [[jotaro-seo-content-strategy]] which recommends FAQ sections for traditional SEO).
