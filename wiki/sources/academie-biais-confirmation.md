# Biais de confirmation — Summary

**One-liner:** People seek/retain info that confirms existing beliefs; align proof with what the prospect already wants to believe to speed the decision (ethically).

## Key takeaways
- **What:** tendency to seek, interpret and retain info confirming prior beliefs.
- **Why it sells:** reassuring validation ("others like me succeeded"), faster decision (less uncertainty), feeling of safety ("logical, safe choice").
- **How to use:** pick proof aligned with the target's beliefs; repeat key arguments across titles/bullets; adopt an angle that starts from what they already believe; lean on external proof (experts, studies, labels).
- **Ethics:** not manipulation — sincerely show how the offer matches their expectations; don't distort facts.
