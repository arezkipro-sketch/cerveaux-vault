# Copywriting Frameworks 2026 — Summary

**One-liner:** A catalogue of ~16 proven copywriting frameworks (AIDA, PAS, FAB, PASTOR, 4 C's, BAB, etc.) for turning objections into conversions across ads, landing pages, emails and e-commerce.

## Key takeaways
- **FAB (Features → Advantages → Benefits)** is the framework named "perfect for e-commerce": features = technical specs, advantages = what it does/how it works (+ competitor comparison), benefits = what the buyer *gets* and how it changes their life. "People don't buy a product, they buy solutions, a transformation, a pain relief."
- **AIDA — Desire step explicitly says: "Add bullet points to better highlight the benefits of your product and make it more desirable."** Bullets are a benefit-amplification device, not a spec dump.
- **The 4 C's** = copy must be Clear, Concise, Compelling, Credible. "Write like people talk", avoid jargon, go straight to the point.
- **Conviction / Proof** appears in AIDCA, ACCA, AIDPPC, 4 P's: skeptical buyers need social proof, data, testimonials before they act.
- **CTA rules:** start with a strong verb (Buy, Save, Download), use second person ("Download Your Free Ebook"), button color must contrast.

## Notable claims (with locations)
- "People don't buy a product, they buy solutions, a transformation, a pain relief." — *(FAB section)*
- "Add bullet points to better highlight the benefits of your product." — *(AIDA / Desire)*
- 80/20 rule (PASTOR): 80% about the reader's problem + others' transformation, 20% about your offer. — *(PASTOR / Offer)*
- BAB example uses concrete data: "Tested on 57 women for 30 days with 89% reduction" — numbers make claims credible. — *(BAB)*
