# FRW Ep. 284: Outsourcing Pitching — Summary

**One-liner:** Nicki reframes client-finding as a task that can be outsourced, exploring agency models, copywriter collectives, and the math of buying back your time.

## Key takeaways
- Pitching is a high-level skill requiring strategy — don't outsource to cheap VAs or AI (produces "crap")
- Three scaling models: (1) build a small agency, take a percentage for finding work; (2) form a collective with fellow copywriters and share a skilled pitcher; (3) hire a CCA student who enjoys pitching strategy
- If billing $75/hr and you can hire a pitcher at $25–$50/hr, the math likely works
- Quarterly business review: evaluate what you love vs. loathe — stop living with pain points
- Key math check: ensure pitched projects earn more than the pitcher costs
- The shift: from "freelancer" mindset to "business owner" mindset

## Notable claims
- "When pitching is done well, the way that we teach our students to do it, it is a direct line to clients." — Kate *(episode)*
- "You do not want to be outsourcing your pitching to a $7 an hour overseas VA… pitching is a skill." — Nicki *(episode)*
- CCA has taught 12,000+ students *(episode intro)*
