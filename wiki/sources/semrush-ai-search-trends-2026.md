# AI Search Trends 2026 — Summary

**One-liner:** Five AI search trends for 2026: queries are getting longer/more complex (avg 8 words), multimodal search exploding (Google Lens 12B visual searches/month), Gen Z adoption at nearly 2× the rate of 30+, CTR dropping sharply when AI summaries appear (only 1% click links in AI summaries), and AI Overviews now trigger for 13.1% of searches (up from 6.49% in Jan 2025).

## Key takeaways

### Trend 1: Queries longer and more complex
- Traditional SEO avg keyword: **4 words**.
- AI prompt avg: **8 words** — 2× longer.
- Users ask complex multi-part questions; AI returns single synthesized responses combining data from multiple sources.
- Optimization: scenario-based pages, natural language headings, prompt research tools.

### Trend 2: Multimodal search exploding
- Google Lens: **12 billion visual searches/month**.
- AI search combines text, images, voice, video inputs.
- AI outputs increasingly structured: tables, bullet lists, step-by-step — more interactive than traditional.
- Optimization: include transcripts (video/audio), alt text (images), captions — so AI can parse non-text content.

### Trend 3: Gen Z adoption leading
- **58 % of US adults under 30** use ChatGPT (vs ~30 % for 30+; nearly 2× rate — Pew Research 2025).
- Gen Z treats AI tools as default search/learning interface.
- Implication for brands targeting younger audience: AI presence more critical earlier.

### Trend 4: CTR declining sharply
| Source | Finding |
|---|---|
| Amsive | CTR dropped 15.5% for queries triggering AI Overviews |
| Pew Research | Clicks nearly 2× higher when NO AI summary appears (15% vs 8%) |
| Pew Research | Only **1% of users** click links INSIDE AI summaries |

- Traffic alone no longer = visibility.
- New success metric: citation frequency + brand mention share + brand search volume growth.

### Trend 5: AI Overviews expanding trigger rate
- Jan 2025: **6.49 %** of searches triggered AI Overviews.
- Mar 2025: **13.1 %** — doubled in 2 months.
- Triggers: complex/multi-part queries, educational and product comparison searches, news-rich topics.
- AI summaries cite 3-8 sources; direct + extractable answers win.
