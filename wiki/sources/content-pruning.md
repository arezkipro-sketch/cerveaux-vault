# Élagage de contenu (Content Pruning) — Summary

**One-liner:** Content pruning = analyzing and removing content that adds no real value (like pruning dead branches) to raise a site's average quality, concentrate authority, fix cannibalization, and save crawl budget — quality over quantity for 2025.

## Key takeaways
- **What to prune:** outdated articles, thin content, duplicate/near-duplicate pages, empty technical pages (tags, empty categories). Goal: improve average quality of existing content, not just add more.
- **Why** → 4 benefits:
  - **Overall SEO:** removing low performers clarifies thematic positioning + concentrates authority on important URLs.
  - **Topical authority:** fixes [[keyword-cannibalization]] — "3 strong articles > 10 mediocre on the same theme."
  - **Crawl budget:** fewer useless URLs → Googlebot focuses on strategic pages → [[crawl-budget]].
  - **UX:** simpler navigation, editorial coherence, more time on site, trust.

## 6-step process (with locations)
1. **Deep content audit** (Screaming Frog, Ahrefs, Semrush, GSC) → 2. **Analyze metrics** (organic sessions GA, impressions/clicks GSC, backlinks/authority Ahrefs/Moz, time-on-page/bounce) → 3. **Categorize**: keep+optimize / update / delete-or-redirect → 4. **Redirection strategy** (301 to similar pages; avoid 404s) → 5. **Update content mapping** (internal links, menus, sitemap) → 6. **Monitor post-pruning KPIs** (organic traffic, indexed pages, avg position, crawl time, keyword gains/losses) *(§Comment procéder)*.

## Best practices (with locations)
- Don't delete too much at once; don't rely on traffic alone (a low-traffic page may have backlinks/conversions); quality first; document actions; review 1-2×/year; combine pruning + updating; watch technical impact (broken links) *(§Bonnes pratiques)*.
