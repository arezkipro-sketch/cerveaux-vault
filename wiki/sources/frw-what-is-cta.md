# What's a CTA — Summary

**One-liner:** A CTA tells the reader exactly what to do next; it's a command (starts with a verb), and which one you pick depends on the funnel stage and medium.

## Key takeaways
- **Definition:** the copy telling the reader the next action; almost every piece has one. Crucial to [[direct-response-copywriting]] (alongside benefits-vs-features).
- **Funnel stage dictates strength:** top-of-funnel (just learning) → *soft* CTA ("Learn more"); bottom-of-funnel (trust built) → *direct* CTA ("Buy now", "Get started", "Sign up").
- **Medium matters:** digital → link text or button (no "Visit websitename.com", just "Visit us"); buttons can significantly raise CTR vs text links (but test). Print needs a typeable short URL.
- **Clarity of next step:** the click must lead exactly where promised; don't make the reader think about the step or the benefit.
- **Always a verb / command:** "Sign up now", "Buy now", "Like us".
- **Test:** wording ("Try it free" vs "Get started"), urgency ("now"/"today"), length, POV (2nd person "Get your free copy" vs 1st person "Yes, I want my free copy"). Keep design constant for true A/B.
- **"Click here" is antiquated:** users tap/swipe/voice; "click" isn't the real action — "Start your membership" > "To start… click here".
- Let the CTA stand out as its own line in the copy doc, not buried in body copy.
