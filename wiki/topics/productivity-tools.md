# Productivity Tools: Trello vs Notion vs Obsidian

**Thesis:** No single winner — choose by how you think: boards (Trello), databases/workspace (Notion), or linked notes (Obsidian). → [[academie-trello-notion-obsidian]]

## Comparison
| Tool | Model | Best for | Watch-out |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trello** | Kanban lists/cards | Task pipelines, editorial calendar, client collab | Limited for deep docs/notes |
| **Notion** | All-in-one workspace/DB | Docs, wikis, databases, dashboards | Steeper learning curve |
| **Obsidian** | Interconnected markdown notes | Networked knowledge, backlinks, local files | Less turnkey for kanban/DB |

## Relations
- **Obsidian powers this very vault** → [[persistent-wiki]] (the LLM-maintained wiki pattern); Trello/Notion suit the [[content-calendar]] + [[client-acquisition]] workflows.
- Sits in [[marketing-tools-landscape]] (organisation category).

## Tensions / open questions
- Often used together (e.g. Trello for tasks + Obsidian for knowledge), not strictly either/or.
