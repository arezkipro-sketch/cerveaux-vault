# Copywriting B2B vs B2C

**Thesis:** Same goal — convert — but the levers diverge with the buyer. B2B sells to rational deciders over a long collective cycle; B2C sells to individuals on emotion over a short cycle.

## Comparison

| Axis | B2B | B2C |
|---|---|---|
| **Target** | Companies, deciders, buyers | Individual consumers |
| **Tone** | Professional, rational, factual | Direct, empathetic, emotional |
| **Lever** | Value, ROI, reliability | Emotion, desire, urgency |
| **Format** | Long, detailed (case studies, white papers) | Short, impactful (emails, ads, sales pages) |
| **Decision cycle** | Long, collective | Fast, individual |
| **Leans on** | [[social-proof]], [[e-e-a-t]], data | [[emotional-copywriting]], [[scarcity-urgency]] |

## Both share
Hook early, benefits over features, guide to action. Differ only in *which* lever leads. Maps to two of the five [[copywriting-types]] (technical/B2B vs emotional/B2C).

## Tensions / open questions
- Clean binary; hybrid B2B2C and "human-to-human" B2B (emotion in B2B) not addressed by the source.
