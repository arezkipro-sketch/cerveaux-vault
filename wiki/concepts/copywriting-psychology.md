# Copywriting Psychology

**Definition:** The use of emotional drivers, cognitive biases and social influence to move a reader toward a purchase. Buyers decide emotionally, then justify logically.

## What we know

### Core buying motivations
- Prevent / ease pain, give pleasure, gain social status or acceptance. Copy should name one. → [[haddington-copywriting-psychology]]

### The 3 buying emotions (sequence)
- **Security → Desire → Urgency:** reassure (guarantees, testimonials) → project a concrete better future (dopamine) → real FOMO to act now. Reproduces the brain's natural decision path; see [[emotional-copywriting]]. → [[academie-3-emotions-achat]]

### Named cognitive biases
- Scarcity, authority, reciprocity, social proof, **commitment & consistency** (small initial yes → logical progression). Full set in [[cognitive-biases]]. → [[academie-5-biais-cognitifs]]

### Persuasion levers
- **Social proof:** testimonials, reviews, counts ("Join 60,000+ customers"). → [[haddington-copywriting-psychology]]
- **Scarcity / FOMO:** limited stock, countdowns, "ends at midnight" — "scarcity heightens perceived value".
- **Reciprocity:** give value upfront (free guide, voucher) → subtle obligation to engage.
- **Risk reduction:** "Essai gratuit", "Sans engagement", "Annulable à tout moment" — lowers perceived risk at the decision point (esp. in CTAs). → [[academie-cta-2025]]
- **Power words:** discover, unleash, proven, limited — spark curiosity + urgency.
- **Specific numbers** make claims credible ("97% reported healthier skin in 30 days"). Links to [[citation-absorption]] (stats +61% influence).

### Emotion over features
- "Less about the product itself, more about how it makes them feel." Features must be translated into feeling. → [[haddington-copywriting-psychology]]

### Ethics (hard rule)
- Never exaggerate; acknowledge limitations; promise only achievable results. Buyers detect manipulation; deceptive claims wreck the brand. Aligns with [[e-e-a-t]].
