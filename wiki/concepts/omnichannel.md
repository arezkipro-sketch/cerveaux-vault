# Omnichannel

**Definition:** A strategy that integrates all of a brand's communication, sales, and relationship channels (physical + digital) into one unified, seamless customer experience — history and preferences follow the customer across every touchpoint → [[omnichannel]].

## Three pillars
- **Data integration** — real-time shared data, a unified DB recognizing the customer anywhere → [[omnichannel]].
- **Channel connection** — channels talk to each other; a journey started on web can finish in app or store → [[omnichannel]].
- **Customer-experience priority** — the customer, not the channel, is the center → [[omnichannel]].

## Omnichannel vs multichannel vs single channel
- **Omnichannel** = highest integration; interconnected, seamless.
- **Multichannel** = present on many channels but **disconnected** (re-explain each time → friction).
- **Single channel** = one channel only; limited reach/flexibility.
→ [[omnichannel]]

## What we know
- Enablers: CRM, marketing automation, AI, data analysis → [[omnichannel]].
- Benefits: better CX, higher conversion, loyalty/retention, centralized data, competitive edge → [[omnichannel]].
- Build steps: map journey → integrate systems/data → personalize → unify service → train teams → monitor/optimize → [[omnichannel]].
- **70%+ of consumers use multiple channels** in their journey (HBR, 46,000 shoppers) → [[omnichannel]].
