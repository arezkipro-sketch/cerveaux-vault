# Brand Strategy (Stratégie de marque)

**Definition:** The structured plan defining how a company wants to be perceived — purpose, values, mission, [[brand-identity]] (visual + verbal), tone, and market positioning. It shapes the whole customer experience at every touchpoint and must align with business objectives → [[strategie-de-marque]].

## Why it matters
- Differentiate on **value not price**; coherence/credibility; consumer trust; attract talent/partners. "A brand without a strategy is just one more brand" → [[strategie-de-marque]].

## 4 brand-architecture types
- **Product brand** — independent identity per product (P&G: Gillette/Ariel/Pantene).
- **Range/extension ("par portée")** — decline the core brand for segments (Coca-Cola Zero/Light).
- **Umbrella ("ombrelle")** — one brand over many products (Apple: iPhone/iPad/MacBook).
- **Endorsement ("caution")** — parent endorses sub-brands (Nestlé → Nescafé/Nesquik).
→ [[strategie-de-marque]]

## 5-step build
1. Mission/vision/values → 2. Market + competition analysis ([[competitor-analysis]], positioning) → 3. [[brand-identity]] (visual + verbal) → 4. Content strategy + digital marketing ([[social-media-strategy]]) → 5. Brand team/agency → [[strategie-de-marque]].

## Relations

- Step 4 ties to [[social-media-strategy]] / [[content-calendar]]; consistent identity supports [[omnichannel]] + [[search-everywhere-optimization]].

## Tensions / open questions
