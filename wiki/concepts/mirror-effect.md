# Mirror Effect (Voice of Customer)

**Definition:** Reflecting the audience's own words, emotions and story back to them so the reader thinks "this person understands exactly my situation". Achieved by observing and rephrasing real language precisely — never inventing. "Vendre sans forcer" by being accurate, not pushy. → [[academie-effet-miroir]]

## What we know
- **Does 3 things:** shows you understand the lived problem, reduces mental effort, raises emotional impact. → [[academie-effet-miroir]]
- **3 levels:** lexical (reuse exact recurring words) → emotional (phrase the real feeling) → narrative (tell the prospect's typical journey). → [[academie-effet-miroir]]
- **Sources of real words (VoC):** public comments (YouTube/LinkedIn/Reddit/forums/groups), client calls (goldmine — exact phrases), recurring questions. → [[academie-effet-miroir]]
- **Method:** collect ~30 real phrases (frustrations/desires/objections/situations/emotional words) → pick 1 person + 1 situation → 3-line intro (problem→feeling→clarity promise) → proof of understanding → mirror the CTA too. → [[academie-effet-miroir]]
- **4 mistakes:** too-literal copy-paste, projecting assumptions, overdoing it (manipulative), sacrificing fluidity. → [[academie-effet-miroir]]

## Relations
- The voice-of-customer research layer feeding [[hook]], [[headline]], [[frustration-desire]], [[call-to-action]].
- Operationalises the "precise audience understanding" element of [[copywriting]]; pairs with [[confirmation bias|cognitive-biases]] (start from their beliefs).
