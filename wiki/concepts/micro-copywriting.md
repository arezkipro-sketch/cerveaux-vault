# Micro-copywriting

**Definition:** The tiny interface texts — buttons, field labels, error/success messages, hints, placeholders — often the last friction point before a conversion. → [[academie-micro-copywriting]]

## What we know
- **Why each word matters:** reduces hesitation, guides the next action, builds trust (human error messages), reinforces brand voice. → [[academie-micro-copywriting]]
- **Examples:** buttons ("Essayer gratuitement"), reassuring field hints ("Nous ne partagerons jamais votre adresse"), human errors ("Oups, cette adresse semble invalide…" > "Erreur"), onboarding steps, ethical scarcity micro-labels. → [[academie-micro-copywriting]]
- **6 principles:** clarity first, concision (1-2 lines), action verbs, empathy (answer the user's in-moment fear), brand-consistent tone, test & optimise. → [[academie-micro-copywriting]]
- **Mistakes:** vague ("Soumettre" < "Envoyer ma demande"), pointless jargon, ignoring context, overloading. → [[academie-micro-copywriting]]

## Relations
- The smallest-scale [[call-to-action]] (button text), paired with [[color-psychology]] (button colour).
- Empathetic error copy = [[mirror-effect]] at micro scale; serves [[landing-page]] / [[sales-funnel]] checkout friction.
