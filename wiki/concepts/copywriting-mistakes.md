# Copywriting Mistakes

**Definition:** The recurring errors — at the strategy level (beginner traps) and the word level (hollow clichés) — that slow progress and wreck credibility. → [[academie-7-erreurs-debutant]], [[academie-10-expressions-bannir]]

## What we know
### Strategy-level (beginner) traps → [[academie-7-erreurs-debutant]]
- Learning everything at once · skipping audience research · copying without understanding · writing for self not reader · forgetting the CTA · not practising regularly · self-teaching with no feedback.

### Word-level clichés to ban → [[academie-10-expressions-bannir]]
- "Opportunité à ne pas manquer", "Résultats garantis", "Meilleur du marché", "Pour tout le monde", "Satisfaction garantie", "Le secret pour réussir", "Produit miracle", "Jamais vu", "Offre exceptionnelle", "Cliquez ici" — each replaced by concrete, specific, provable copy.
- **Principle:** ban the vague, favour the concrete (precision, proof, emotional connection).

## Relations
- Inverse of the whole copywriting cluster: skipping research = ignoring [[mirror-effect]]; writing for self = ignoring benefits ([[copywriting-frameworks]]); no CTA = ignoring [[call-to-action]]; vague claims = breaking [[ethical-copywriting]] / [[copywriting-psychology]] honesty.

## Tensions / open questions
- "Self-teaching is a mistake" is partly the publisher's sales pitch for paid training.
