# Brand Authority (copywriter)

**Definition:** The set of signals that make a prospect think "this person knows what they're doing — I can trust them". Winners inspire trust, not just write well; authority lowers perceived risk → faster sales + higher rates. → [[academie-autorite-copywriting]]

## What we know
- **4 signals:** clarity (understand what you do) + proof (you've done it) + process (how you work) + consistency (same quality everywhere). → [[academie-autorite-copywriting]]
- **Why:** reduces risk (decide faster), raises rates at equal skill (perceived value), attracts good clients / repels bad. → [[academie-autorite-copywriting]]
- **6 pillars:** clear positioning (channel/market/format/problem) · demonstration not assertion · decision-oriented portfolio · qualitative social proof · "proof content" · network. → [[academie-autorite-copywriting]]
- **5 killers:** no through-line, illegible offer, perfectionism, copying, not showing work. → [[academie-autorite-copywriting]]
- **14-day plan:** positioning+offer → MVP portfolio → demonstrative content → outreach. → [[academie-autorite-copywriting]]

## Relations
- The authority bias of [[cognitive-biases]] applied to the copywriter's *own* brand; raises [[value-anchoring]] perceived value.
- Built on [[usp]] (positioning), [[social-proof]] (qualitative testimonials), avoids [[copywriting-mistakes]] (the 5 killers); also = [[e-e-a-t]] for a personal brand.
- Personal-brand analogue of company [[brand-strategy]] (same logic: positioning + identity + consistency build trust).
