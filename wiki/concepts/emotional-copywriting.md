# Emotional Copywriting

**Definition:** Writing to trigger a *useful* emotion, then guiding the reader to a calm, logical decision. "L'émotion ouvre la porte; la preuve et la clarté la referment." Not manipulation — making the decision easier. → [[academie-copywriting-emotionnel]]

## What we know
- **Core sequence:** **Émotion → Clarté → Preuve → Action.** Emotion drives attention/memory/action; logic then verifies (credible? fit? risky? now?). → [[academie-copywriting-emotionnel]]
- **The 3 buying emotions, in order:** **security** (guarantees, testimonials → lower anxiety) → **desire** (project a concrete better future → dopamine) → **urgency** (real FOMO → act now). Reproduces the brain's decision path. → [[academie-3-emotions-achat]]
- **6 levers:** healthy fear, desire, relief, belonging, pride/identity, curiosity (easiest entry). → [[academie-copywriting-emotionnel]]
- **Message architecture:** hook on lived experience → measured agitation (precision, not drama) → sensory solution projection → proof + risk reduction → one obvious CTA. → [[academie-copywriting-emotionnel]]
- **Method:** pick ONE dominant emotion → precise "vous" hook → micro-scene → concrete "after" → micro-proofs throughout → one simple next step. → [[academie-copywriting-emotionnel]]
- **Mistakes:** vague promise, over-dramatising, stacked bonuses, no proof, multiple CTAs, "marketing" tone. → [[academie-copywriting-emotionnel]]

## Relations
- The applied, emotion-first face of [[copywriting-psychology]]; one of the 5 [[copywriting-types]].
- Uses [[storytelling]] (micro-scene), [[hook]] (lived-experience opener), [[copywriting-frameworks]] (PAS agitation), [[call-to-action]] (single CTA), and value-anchoring.
- Desire = FAB Benefit; urgency = [[cognitive-biases]] scarcity.

## Tensions / open questions
- "One single CTA" here vs "repeat the CTA" in [[call-to-action]] → resolve as: repeat the *same* action, never offer competing choices.
