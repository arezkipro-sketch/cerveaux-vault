# Hook / Opening Line

**Definition:** The first impression after the headline — a punchy phrase or concept whose *only* job is to make the reader want to read the next line. The title earns the click; the hook decides whether the reader stays. → [[academie-hook-accroche]], [[academie-capter-attention-premiere-ligne]]

## What we know
- **Must produce ≥1 effect:** feel concerned, curiosity, tension, visible benefit, trust. Else the reader leaves. → [[academie-capter-attention-premiere-ligne]]
- **4 traits:** clarity, impact (strong emotion), relevance to the offer, originality. → [[academie-hook-accroche]]
- **Hook forms:** intriguing question, shocking statistic, captivating story, strong promise. → [[academie-hook-accroche]]
- **11 first-line techniques:** direct "vous" · punchy question · curiosity loop · contradict a received idea · concrete promise · precise symptom · blunt truth · micro-story · stakes/cost of inaction · before/after contrast · mini-test. → [[academie-capter-attention-premiere-ligne]]
- **Opening-paragraph model:** Hook → Symptom → Consequence → Promise → Transition. → [[academie-capter-attention-premiere-ligne]]
- **Build method:** identify audience's main desire/pain → craft a phrase stirring emotion → lead into the sales message. → [[academie-hook-accroche]]
- **Mistakes:** vague/generic, lying/over-promising, ignoring audience, empty phrases ("Aujourd'hui nous allons voir…"), too formal.
