# Frustration & Desire (buying engine)

**Definition:** The universal conversion engine — a purchase happens when the prospect feels the gap between **now** (frustration) and the **wanted future** (desire). The bigger the felt gap, the more tension, the more likely the action. → [[academie-frustration-desir]]

## What we know
- **Frustration = exit energy** (push from a repeated problem); **Desire = pull energy** (attraction to a concrete better state). The copywriter's job: put precise words on both. → [[academie-frustration-desir]]
- **Use frustration honestly:** name the exact, lived frustration in the prospect's words → prove understanding → show real impact (no drama) → expose the true cause of past failure → install your angle. → [[academie-frustration-desir]]
- **Activate desire:** make the improved situation visualisable, concrete/measurable, and proof-backed (else it reads as empty marketing). → [[academie-frustration-desir]]
- **6-step arc:** Frustration → Impact → Cause → Desire → Bridge (method) → Action. → [[academie-frustration-desir]]
- **3 mistakes:** too much frustration / too little desire; desire too big & not credible; no concrete bridge. → [[academie-frustration-desir]]
