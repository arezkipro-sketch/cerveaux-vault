# USP (Unique Selling Proposition)

**Definition:** Not a clever tagline but a *fixed decision* that makes you legible (not unique) in a saturated market — "le marché ne vous rejette pas, il vous confond". One sentence that makes the right people choose you. → [[jeremy-allard-usp]]

## What we know
- **The problem is indecision, not confidence** — interchangeable "good" profiles confuse the market. A USP plants a stake in the ground. → [[jeremy-allard-usp]]
- **8-step method:** pen+paper 3-min freewrite ("why choose ME today") → list everything, strike the 80% a competitor could say verbatim → name the *real unspoken* painful problem → link your difference to that problem → compress to one cutting sentence **"J'aide [profil] à [résultat] sans [frustration]"** (no "et", no soft adjectives) → test on ONE real person → place it where it sells → visualise a first client saying "c'est exactement ça". → [[jeremy-allard-usp]]
- **Mantra:** exclude part of the market to be chosen by the right people. → [[jeremy-allard-usp]]

## Relations
- The "real painful problem" step = [[frustration-desire]] + [[mirror-effect]] (the client's unspoken words).
- Underpins [[value-anchoring]] (categorisation/positioning), the [[about-page]], and the headline promise ([[headline]]).
- A positioning foundation for [[copywriting]] overall; the individual-level counterpart of company [[brand-strategy]] positioning ("differentiate on value not price" → [[strategie-de-marque]]).
