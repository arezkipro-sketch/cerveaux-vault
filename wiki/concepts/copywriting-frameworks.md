# Copywriting Frameworks

**Definition:** Reusable structural formulas for persuasive copy. Each sequences the reader from attention to action. They overlap; pick by format (ad, landing page, email, product page) and buyer awareness stage.

## What we know

### FAB — Features / Advantages / Benefits (the e-commerce one)
- **Feature:** the technical characteristic ("PVC transparent", "coque ABS").
- **Advantage:** what it does / how it works ("s'essuie d'un coup d'éponge"); can include competitor comparison.
- **Benefit:** what the buyer *gets* and how life changes ("retrouve son rouge à lèvres en un regard, sans fouiller").
- Core law: "People don't buy a product, they buy solutions, a transformation, a pain relief." → [[copywriting-frameworks-2026]]
- **Format fit:** product sheets + commercial pitches. **5-step process** (→ [[academie-methode-fab]]): list features → attach an advantage to each → translate to benefit → adapt vocabulary to the avatar → embed in pages/emails/CTAs. Keep to **3-5 strongest FAB**, benefit-first, each backed by proof. Never skip the Advantage (logic gap) or promise unrealistic benefits (distrust).

### AIDA — Attention / Interest / Desire / Action
- Desire step: **"Add bullet points to better highlight the benefits of your product."** Bullets = benefit amplifier.
- Action step: urgency + guarantee + clear CTA. → [[copywriting-frameworks-2026]]
- **Format fit:** best for sales pages + long ads. Worked example: open on a striking number (A), explain why most fail alone (I), show the fix (D), immediate signup (A). → [[academie-aida-pas-fab]]

### PAS — Problem / Agitate / Solution
- Simplest high-impact frame: name the problem, stress it emotionally, present the product as the fix.
- **Format fit:** best for short punchy messages — emails, ads, posts. Opener example: "Vous perdez du temps à écrire sans résultats?" → [[academie-aida-pas-fab]]

### The 4 C's — Clear, Concise, Compelling, Credible
- Clear: no jargon, "write like people talk". Concise: no filler. Compelling: emotional/story. Credible: testimonials + data. → [[copywriting-frameworks-2026]]

### Proof / Conviction (AIDCA, ACCA, AIDPPC, 4 P's)
- Skeptical buyers need social proof, data, infographics, testimonials before action.

### CTA rules
- Strong verb first (Buy, Save, Download), second person ("Secure Your Spot"), high-contrast button. → [[copywriting-frameworks-2026]]
