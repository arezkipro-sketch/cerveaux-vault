# Copywriting

**Definition:** The craft of writing persuasive text that moves a reader to a specific action — buy, subscribe, download, request a quote. It optimises for **conversion** and an emotional link with the reader, distinguishing it from plain content/editorial writing whose goal is to inform. → [[academie-copywriting-definition]]

## What we know
- **Goal = action, not just information.** Every piece targets one desired action; success is measured by conversion. → [[academie-copywriting-definition]]
- **Persuasion arc.** Good copy must grab attention fast, spark interest with a targeted message, build emotional connection, then drive action — the AIDA skeleton. → [[academie-copywriting-definition]]
- **Four elements of effective copy:** punchy headline, precise audience understanding, clear benefits over features, direct CTA. → [[academie-copywriting-definition]]
- **Common formats:** sales page (problem → solution → benefits), marketing email (e.g. abandoned-cart + limited offer), social ad (hook question). → [[academie-copywriting-definition]]
- **Learned by** studying persuasion, analysing high-performing examples, and deliberate practice. → [[academie-copywriting-definition]]
