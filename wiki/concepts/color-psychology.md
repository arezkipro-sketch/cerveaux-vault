# Color Psychology (in copy/CRO)

**Definition:** Colour acts as a psychological stimulus — up to ~90% of a first impression is colour-linked *(unsourced)*. Used strategically it triggers emotion, builds trust/urgency, and guides the eye to the CTA. → [[academie-couleurs-achat]]

## What we know
- **Meanings:** red = urgency/energy · blue = trust/professional (B2B/finance) · green = growth/health/nature · yellow = optimism (accent) · purple = luxury/creativity · orange = friendly decision-nudge · black = premium/elegance · white = simplicity/breathing room. Vary by culture. → [[academie-couleurs-achat]]
- **4 principles:** align with brand identity; visual hierarchy (primary + secondary + contrast for CTAs); accessibility/contrast; multichannel consistency. → [[academie-couleurs-achat]]
- **CTAs:** contrasting colour so the button pops; same colour for all primary CTAs (build a reflex); pair with punchy [[micro-copywriting]]. A/B test. → [[academie-couleurs-achat]]
- **Mistakes:** too many colours (limit 2-3), ignoring culture, copying competitors, not testing. → [[academie-couleurs-achat]]

## Relations
- Supports [[call-to-action]] (contrast) and [[landing-page]] (visual hierarchy); warm = [[scarcity-urgency]], cool = [[social-proof]]/trust.
