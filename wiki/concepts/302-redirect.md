# 302 Redirect

**Definition:** A *temporary* redirect (HTTP 302 Found) sending users/bots to another page while preserving the original page's keyword rankings and link value. → [[semrush-302-redirect]]

## What we know
- **302 = temporary, 301 = permanent.** Use 302 for genuinely temporary moves: A/B tests, maintenance, under-construction, seasonal/regional swaps — the original URL keeps its ranking. → [[semrush-302-redirect]]
- Improves UX (no broken/under-construction landings). → [[semrush-302-redirect]]
- **Mistake:** 302 for a *permanent* move — link equity doesn't fully consolidate to the new URL; use [[301-redirect]] instead. → [[semrush-302-redirect]]

## Relations
- The temporary counterpart of [[301-redirect]]; both are 3xx codes in [[http-status-codes-seo]]; governs [[link-juice]] transfer.

## Tensions / open questions
- A 302 left in place long-term may eventually be treated as a 301 by Google.
