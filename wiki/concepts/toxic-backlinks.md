# Toxic Backlinks

**Definition:** Inbound links placed for ranking manipulation (not user benefit) that violate Google's link-spam guidelines and can hurt visibility. Usually devalued; in volume they can trigger a manual action/penalty. → [[semrush-toxic-backlinks]]

## What we know
- **9 causes:** paying for follow links, excessive reciprocal exchanges, PBNs, link bots, unnatural forum/comment links, low-quality directories, widget links, contract-mandated links, + automated bulk schemes. → [[semrush-toxic-backlinks]]
- **Qualify links:** `rel="nofollow"` (ignore), `rel="sponsored"` (paid), `rel="ugc"` (user-generated) — for ads, gifted products, influencers, paid directories, widgets. → [[semrush-toxic-backlinks]]
- **Remediate:** assess toxicity, request removal, or disavow — but Google often simply ignores bad links, so don't over-disavow. → [[semrush-toxic-backlinks]]

## Relations
- The risk side of [[backlinks]] / [[off-page-seo]]; overlaps [[black-hat-seo]] (PBN, link schemes) and [[jotaro-seo-pbn]].
- nofollow/sponsored/ugc attributes affect [[link-juice]] flow.

## Tensions / open questions
- "Toxicity Score" is a Semrush proprietary metric; aggressive disavowing can do more harm than good.
