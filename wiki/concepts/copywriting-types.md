# Copywriting Types

**Definition:** Copywriting is not one skill but several approaches, each tied to a primary objective. Choose by goal; a professional switches between them. → [[academie-5-types-copywriting]]

## What we know
- **Direct response** — drive immediate action (buy, signup, click). Channels: sales pages, marketing emails, online ads. Strength: directly measurable in conversions. → [[academie-5-types-copywriting]]
- **SEO copywriting** — content optimised for search engines *and* readers; strategic keywords + adapted structure. Goal: qualified organic traffic, long-term acquisition. Overlaps the SEO cluster ([[keyword-research]], [[article-structure]]). → [[academie-5-types-copywriting]]
- **Branding** — build a strong, memorable identity rather than sell now; slogans, ad storytelling. Strength: differentiation + long-term loyalty. → [[academie-5-types-copywriting]]
- **Technical / B2B** — vulgarise complex info to convince rational deciders; case studies, technical product sheets. Persuasion by logic + credibility. → [[academie-5-types-copywriting]]
- **Emotional** — play on feelings and desires; strong in B2C, drives impulse buys (perfume ad, charity appeal). → [[academie-5-types-copywriting]]

### Selection rule
Sell fast → direct · attract traffic → SEO · build brand → branding · convince B2B → technical · emotional connection → emotional.

## Relations
- A sub-classification of the base craft [[copywriting]].
- "Emotional" type is powered by [[copywriting-psychology]]; "direct response" leans on [[copywriting-frameworks]] (AIDA/PAS).
- "SEO copywriting" sits at the boundary explored in [[copywriting-vs-redaction-web]].

## Tensions / open questions
- The "SEO copywriting" type vs treating web writing as a separate discipline — see [[copywriting-vs-redaction-web]].
- Branding ROI is hard to measure vs the directly-measurable direct-response type.
