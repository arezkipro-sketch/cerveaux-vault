# Content Pruning (Élagage de contenu)

**Definition:** Analyzing and removing (or merging/redirecting) content that adds no real value — outdated, thin, duplicate, or empty pages — to raise a site's average quality, like pruning dead branches so strong ones grow → [[content-pruning]].

## Why
- **Overall SEO:** clarifies thematic positioning; concentrates authority on important URLs → [[content-pruning]].
- **Topical authority:** fixes [[keyword-cannibalization]] — "3 strong articles > 10 mediocre" → [[topical-authority]].
- **Crawl budget:** fewer useless URLs → Googlebot focuses on strategic pages → [[crawl-budget]].
- **UX:** simpler navigation, coherence, more time on site, trust → [[content-pruning]].

## 6-step process
1. Deep content [[seo-audit|audit]] (Screaming Frog/Ahrefs/Semrush/GSC) → 2. Analyze metrics (sessions, impressions/clicks, backlinks, time/bounce) → 3. Categorize: keep+optimize / update / delete-or-redirect → 4. **301 redirect** (avoid 404s) → 5. Update content mapping ([[maillage-interne]], menus, [[xml-sitemap]]) → 6. Monitor KPIs (traffic, indexed pages, position, crawl time, keyword gains/losses) → [[content-pruning]].

## Best practices
- Don't delete too much at once; don't judge by traffic alone (backlinks/conversions matter); document; review 1-2×/year; combine pruning + updating → [[content-pruning]].

## Relations
- Maintenance counterpart of content creation; fixes [[keyword-cannibalization]], frees [[crawl-budget]], builds [[topical-authority]].
- "Quality over quantity" aligns with [[e-e-a-t]] + Google's "scaled content abuse" penalty ([[irentdumpsters-google-ai-guide]]).

## Tensions / open questions
- No scoring rubric to weigh traffic vs backlinks vs conversions when deciding to cut.
