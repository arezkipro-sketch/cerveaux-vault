# Social Proof

**Definition:** Showing that others already trusted the offer and got results — answers "why should I believe you?", lowers perceived risk, raises credibility. Place it to kill a specific objection at the right moment. → [[academie-preuve-sociale]]

## What we know
- **Why it works:** under uncertainty humans follow others; reviews influence ~90% of purchase decisions *(unverified stat)*. → [[academie-preuve-sociale]]
- **6 forms:** testimonials/reviews (precise, credible w/ name+role+photo, varied) · case studies + before/after · numbers/stats (verifiable, current) · labels/certifications/media · community/social counts · user-generated proof (DM screenshots, stories — with consent). → [[academie-preuve-sociale]]
- **Placement:** sales/landing (after offer + before final CTA), about page, email sequences, blog, social. → [[academie-preuve-sociale]]
- **Best practice:** tell a story not "C'était super!", stay authentic, vary formats, give context, refresh, get consent (RGPD), tie each proof to a benefit. → [[academie-preuve-sociale]]
- **Mistakes:** generic/fake testimonials, copied proof, overloading ("too much proof kills proof"), ignoring objections, no CTA after proof. → [[academie-preuve-sociale]]
