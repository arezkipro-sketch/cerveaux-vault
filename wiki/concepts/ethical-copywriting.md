# Ethical Copywriting

**Definition:** Persuasion built on trust and transparency rather than manipulation. Trades the one-off spike of manipulative copy for *durable* conversion, retention and brand reputation. → [[projectfork-copywriting-ethique]]

## What we know
- **Ethical vs manipulative:** ethical = high trust, stable/growing conversion, low risk; manipulative = low trust, momentary spike, high reputation risk. Avoiding over-promise improves retention. → [[projectfork-copywriting-ethique]]
- **Principles:** concrete benefits + proof, reassuring microcopy on critical actions, no vague/deceptive claims, clear verifiable CTAs. → [[projectfork-copywriting-ethique]]
- **Across the e-commerce journey:** honest product pages (real dimensions, guarantees, verified reviews), reassuring checkout microcopy (delivery/security/returns → fewer abandons), post-purchase transparency. → [[projectfork-copywriting-ethique]]
- **Measure:** A/B + KPIs (conversion, cart abandon, 30-day retention, AOV); watch post-purchase cancellations, not just sales. → [[projectfork-copywriting-ethique]]

## Relations
- The codified honesty rule running through [[copywriting-psychology]], [[scarcity-urgency]] (real not fake), [[cognitive-biases]] ("not manipulation"), and the cliché-ban in [[copywriting-mistakes]].
- Operationalised via [[micro-copywriting]], [[social-proof]] (verifiable), [[product-bullet-points]]; reduces [[sales-funnel]] checkout friction.
