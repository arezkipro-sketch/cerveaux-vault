# Long-Tail Keyword Strategy

**Definition:** An SEO approach that targets specific, lower-competition keyword phrases (long-tail) instead of — or in addition to — high-volume primary keywords, in order to rank more easily, attract qualified traffic, and improve conversions.

## What we know
- Core rationale: primary keywords are often too competitive to rank quickly; long-tail sub-keywords have smaller audiences but far lower competition → [[jotaro-seo-long-tail-strategy]].
- Example: *café* (highly competitive) → pivot to *tasse de café* (niche, lower competition) → produce content → rank → link to product page → [[jotaro-seo-long-tail-strategy]].
- Key benefits → [[jotaro-seo-long-tail-strategy]]:
  - **Reduced direct competition** — easier SERP penetration.
  - **Qualified traffic** — visitor intent matches the offer.
  - **Higher conversion rate** — traffic is already niche-matched.
  - **Authority building** — becoming a recognized reference on the niche.
- Execution requires identifying both **keyword difficulty** and **search volume** on long-tail targets before committing *(from thread comment, @destiny_boussi)* → [[jotaro-seo-long-tail-strategy]].
- The page structure for long-tail: article / content page (page fille) → links to the main product page (page mère) → forms a [[cocon-semantique]].

## Relations
- Foundation of the [[cocon-semantique]] content architecture.
- Pairs with [[exact-match-domain]] (domain can reflect the niche keyword for extra signal).

## Tensions / open questions
- At what search volume does a keyword stop being "long-tail"? Not defined in this source.
- Long-tail in French SEO markets may behave differently from English markets due to query volume scale.
