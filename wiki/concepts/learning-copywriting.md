# Learning Copywriting

**Definition:** Copywriting is a learnable skill from zero (no innate talent needed); the path is fundamentals → study performers → deliberate daily practice → feedback. → [[academie-apprendre-sans-experience]]

## What we know
- **4 steps:** master fundamentals (persuasion, sales-text structure, headline, storytelling) → study what worked (ads/sales pages/emails) → practise daily with feedback → get accompaniment/mentorship. → [[academie-apprendre-sans-experience]]
- **First clients fast:** direct LinkedIn/email prospecting, communities, showcase samples (even fictional). → [[academie-apprendre-sans-experience]]
- Reinforced by the FRW career angle: start with [[frw-start-copywriting-1-hour|1 hour a day]] and manage [[frw-first-client-panic|first-client panic]]. Persuasion-is-a-skill thesis → [[persuasive-writing]].

## Relations
- Avoids [[copywriting-mistakes]] (beginner traps); builds toward [[brand-authority]] (portfolio) and [[client-acquisition]].
- The "skill not gift" stance = [[persuasive-writing]]; sits under the broader career view of [[ai-vs-human-writing]] (be the strategic writer).

## Tensions / open questions
- Educator sources frame self-teaching as slower (course-pitch bias) — but practice + feedback is genuinely the consensus path.
