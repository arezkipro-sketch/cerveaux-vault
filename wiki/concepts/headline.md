# Headline

**Definition:** The main opening phrase of a marketing piece (sales page, ad, email subject, blog title, about page). It is the "first sale" — the sale of attention — and the single biggest lever on conversion: a weak title means the rest is invisible. → [[academie-7-formules-headlines]]

## What we know
- **3 jobs, in order:** grab attention → promise clear value ("what do I gain, now?") → push to continue (curiosity/desire/contrast/soft urgency). → [[academie-7-formules-headlines]]
- **3 ingredients:** Result (what they want) + Mechanism (how/why possible) + Proof / risk-reduction. → [[academie-7-formules-headlines]]
- **7 reusable formulas:** "Qui d'autre veut [result]?" · "La vérité sur [problem]" · "Comment [result] en [delay] même si [objection]" · "[N] façons de [result]" · "Le moyen le plus simple de [result] sans [sacrifice]" · "Avertissement: ne faites pas [error]…" · "Maintenant vous pouvez [result] grâce à [new method]". → [[academie-7-formules-headlines]]
- **Write method:** define audience (1 sentence) → pick dominant emotion → write 10 titles → keep 3 by filter (specific, credible, reader-oriented "vous", jargon-free). → [[academie-7-formules-headlines]]
- **Test on** click rate, scroll/read time, conversion, micro-actions.
- **Colder traffic = more important headline.** → [[academie-7-formules-headlines]]

## As an SEO title tag
- In [[seo-copywriting]] the headline doubles as the **title tag**: keep **50-60 chars**, keyword early, accurate/clear — it's the SERP "signpost" driving CTR; pair with one H1 (20-70 chars) → [[seo-copywriting]].

## Relations
- Earns the click; hands off to the [[hook]] (first line keeps the reader).
- Is the **Attention** step of AIDA → [[copywriting-frameworks]].
- Emotion selection + specificity/proof draw on [[copywriting-psychology]].
- A sub-technique of [[copywriting]].

## Tensions / open questions
- Formulas/examples are heuristics, not market-tested data — always A/B.
