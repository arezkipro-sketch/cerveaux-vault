# Click-Through Rate (CTR)

**Definition:** The share of people who click after seeing an item (thumbnail, title, ad, SERP result, subject line). A primary engagement/ranking signal: on YouTube, attractive thumbnails and clear titles raise CTR, which the algorithm uses to gauge relevance → [[youtube-algorithme]].

## What we know
- On YouTube, CTR on **thumbnails + titles** is a core ranking signal feeding the [[youtube-algorithm]]; creators should A/B-refine titles and thumbnails using their data → [[youtube-algorithme]].

## CTR par position Google (étude Growthsrc 2025, post-IA Overview)
- Position 1 organique = **19 %** des clics ; les **3 premières positions captent ≈ 43 %** (près de la moitié) → [[410-gone-premier-google-top3]].
- Positions 5 à 9 : taux quasi identiques ; **position 10 rebondit** (plus visible, plus de clics que 7-9).
- **Effet IA Overview** : CTR des premières places en **baisse**, CTR des dernières positions de page 1 en **hausse** → [[ai-overviews]].
- Constante : être premier génère toujours le plus de clics ; être en page 1 reste prioritaire. *(Données requêtes anglophones — transposabilité FR à confirmer.)*

## Relations
- Driven by [[headline]] / [[hook]] craft (the "title" half) and visual design (the "thumbnail" half).
- Feeds [[youtube-algorithm]]; a lever in [[video-marketing]], paid ads ([[display-advertising]]), and email ([[email-copywriting]]).
- A core Google [[navboost]] signal (SERP title/meta = the CTR lever there).

## Tensions / open questions
- High CTR with low retention can backfire (clickbait) — the algorithm also weights watch time/retention.
