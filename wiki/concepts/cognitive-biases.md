# Cognitive Biases (in copywriting)

**Definition:** Mental shortcuts the brain uses to decide quickly. In copy, naming and respecting them makes messages more persuasive — framed as *understanding how the brain works*, not manipulation. → [[academie-5-biais-cognitifs]]

## What we know
- **Scarcity** — rarer = higher perceived value; fear of missing out. Limited quantity, deadlines, early-bird bonuses. Deep-dive → [[scarcity-urgency]]. → [[academie-5-biais-cognitifs]]
- **Authority** — we trust perceived experts. Concrete results, testimonials, citing recognised sources. → [[academie-5-biais-cognitifs]]
- **Reciprocity** — a free gift creates an urge to give back. Free guide/sample/tips before the sale. → [[academie-5-biais-cognitifs]]
- **Social proof** — people validate via others' behaviour. Authentic testimonials, user counts, case studies. Deep-dive → [[social-proof]]. → [[academie-5-biais-cognitifs]]
- **Commitment & consistency** — people stay true to past choices. Ask a small initial commitment, then a logical progression. → [[academie-5-biais-cognitifs]]
- **Confirmation bias** — people seek/retain info confirming prior beliefs. Pick proof aligned with what the target already wants to believe, repeat key arguments, start from their angle → reassurance + faster decision. → [[academie-biais-confirmation]]

## Relations
- A named-bias subset of the broader [[copywriting-psychology]] (which also covers power words, ethics).
