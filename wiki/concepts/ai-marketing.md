# AI in Marketing

**Definition:** Application of [[artificial-intelligence]] / [[machine-learning]] to marketing tasks — the domain where AI shows the "most immediate and measurable" impact, per Neil Patel → [[apercu-ia-guide]].

## What we know
- Core use cases → [[apercu-ia-guide]]:
  - **Audience segmentation** with surgical precision.
  - **Real-time ad-budget optimization**.
  - **Personalized content at scale**.
  - **Lead scoring** — predicting which leads will convert.
  - **Chatbots** handling thousands of conversations, qualifying leads 24/7 (NLP-driven).
- **Hyper-personalization** is becoming the norm: moving from demographic segments to unique per-individual experiences, even real-time ad content generated from browsing behavior → [[apercu-ia-guide]].
- Personalized experiences claimed to lift conversion ~20% → [[apercu-ia-guide]] *(marketing claim, unsourced)*.

## Relations
- Subdomain of [[artificial-intelligence]]; overlaps [[ai-seo]], [[ai-assisted-copywriting]], [[lead-generation]], [[sales-funnel]].
- Feeds the broader [[marketing-tools-landscape]]; powers AI-driven personalization in [[omnichannel]] strategies.

## Tensions / open questions
- ROI figures are self-reported; no independent benchmark.
